What I suggested in an earlier message was that we create a stable branch 
based on an earlier release, that is maintained only with critical fixes, 
while the development branch evolves.

I apologize that I was too subtle in my previous post.

There is nothing wrong with crazy development cycles, as long as users have 
something they can rely on. It is then their choice whether to go for the 
bells and frills, or to stay with the tried and tested. 

Once the development branch has stableized, it will be released and becomes 
the new frozen stable branch.

This is of minimal impact, but provides our customers with the quality they 
need and (their choice) the features they want. Frill seekers will play with 
the devel branch, people who just want to go from A to B the boring way (me) 
can use stable.

Is this a viable compromise for everybody here? Please speak up, and give me 
some feedback.
