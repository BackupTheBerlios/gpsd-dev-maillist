On Sat, 4 Jun 2005, Andreas Brauchli wrote:

> i've found another misbehavior: starting gpsd, opening xgps, closing
> xgps, opening it again causes gpsd to change the speed from 4800 to 9600

This is actually very expected behavior, unless you start xgps with the
- -n flag.  So, simple anser, if you do not like it, then just start
gpsd with -n.

If you start with "-n" then the gpd port is opened once and kept open.

The default (without -n) is to close the gps port when not in use.

The default is to close the port because many gps will shut down, or go
into battery saving mode, when the port is closed.

When you reconnect to the gps port the gps may have gone to sleep or
shut off and therefore needs the full autobaud/init cycle to repeat.

The Garmin USB devices are a good eaxmple of the goodness of -n.  When
the ttyUSB port is shut down the Garmin GPS also shutsi down and save
power.  The downside is that when xgps reconnects to gpsd then gpsd has
to fully re-init the GPS.
