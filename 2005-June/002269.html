Gary E. Miller <gem at rellim.com>:
> What's the easiset way in svn to revert your last change?

svn doesn't really have a revert operation.  It has something called
'revert' but that just replaces the working copy with the local baseline
version (which is the repository copy of the fiule as of the last update).

To do the equivalent of a revert, 

1)  svn -r 2736 garmin.c

This will replace your garmin.c with the one just before my change.  But
if you try 'svn commit' right after that it will complain about an out-of-date
version. So do this:

mv garmin.c goodversion.c
svn up garmin.c			# After this, local base and repository match
mv goodversion.c garmin.c
svn commit
