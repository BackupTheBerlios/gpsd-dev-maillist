That's why data gets a timestamp. You can report it immediately when you get 
it (minimal latency) and repeat it while it's valid. As long as your TO value 
is longer than assumed cycle time you are fine. If your assumed cycle time is 
too short, you get messages missing the timed out components (like now).
If your assumed cycle time is too long, values will be updated before they 
expire, that's what we want!

Failure case: your assumed cycle time is too long, and the data source fails 
without your knowledge -> you will ship messages with the old data till it 
expires, big deal.
