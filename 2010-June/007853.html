I read all the (overall very good) information published on the
project web site. Among other things, i better understand the current
architecture now that i'm aware of the design choices...
Yet i haven't found answers to a few specific, MP-bug related,
questions about the multiplexer inner gears, if you don't mind.

Perhaps it's bets to take an example: let's suppose we have 2 GPS
devices connected, with 2 packets ready to be read() on each serial
line. What should the multiplexer do?
  (a) process 1 packet per device in turn
  (b) process 2 packets from a device, then 2 packets from the other
If i understood correctly, it now attempts to process 1 packet per
device in turn (a). Among other things, one approach to fix the MP bug
implies switching to algorithm (b):
   * Would that be a theoretical problem?
   * Would that be a practical problem (considering what remaining
code might expect from the device polling step)?

As i understand it, packet_get() is used all around in the gpsd
project as a generic fd-to-packet parser, although i kind of remember
you saying it was only used by gps_poll in the gpsd daemon.
How important is it that it stays that way?
