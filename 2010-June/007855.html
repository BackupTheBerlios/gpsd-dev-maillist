Randy <randyqiuxy at hotmail.com>:
> I'm now a little puzzled with some concept about AIS's
> AIVDM/AIVDO(http://gpsd.berlios.de/AIVDM.html#_aivdm_aivdo_payload_armoring).
> 1.question
> I see there are two tables about ASCII,one is ASCII payload armoring and
> another is Sixbit ASCII,then what's the relationship between them and
> how should I decode the characters into sixbit bitfields?

The ASCII payload armoring is how you go from characters in an AIVDM
packet to six-bit segments, which you then concatenate to get the
binary payload of the message.

Sixbit ASCII us how you map six--bit segments within text fields to ASCII 
characters.
