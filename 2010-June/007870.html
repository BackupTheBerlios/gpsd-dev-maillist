David Richfield <davidrichfield at gmail.com>:
> I upgraded to Ubuntu Lucid Lynx, and now gpsd has stopped working.
> 
> I can still do rfcomm bind rfcomm0, and when I say "cat /dev/rfcomm0",
> the blue light on the Nokia LD-4W goes on, and data gets printed to
> the console.
> 
> Before the upgrade, when I started gpsd, the light wouldn't go on
> until I attached a client (e.g. gpxlogger).  Now the light doesn't go
> on at all, and the client doesn't get any data at all.  How do I
> investigate the bug?

Try running gpsd like this:

gpsd -D 5 -n -N /dev/rfcomm0

That starts the daemon in foreground with debugging set high and tells
it not to wait to see a client request before opening the device.  That
will tell you if it can get data from the GPS.
