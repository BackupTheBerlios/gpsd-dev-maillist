On Mon, 10 Jan 2011, Dave Platt wrote:

> On Jan 9, 2011, at 8:08 AM, Håkan Johansson <f96hajo at chalmers.se> wrote:
>
>> 
>> The recent commit disabling prodding of oncore devices makes mine not identify at all on startup after long power-off (just tested).
>
> I suspect you have an Oncore VP?  Those models don't start tracking and
> reporting immediately when they come out of a full power-down state.  They
> just sit there in idle mode, waiting for a command.  You must send them
> a command to initiate tracking and reporting, or some other polled command,
> or they will appear to be inert.
>
> Other Oncore models e.g. the UT series do not have this quirk.

It is actually supposed to be an UT+ (marked R5122U1112), but this 
silent-on-start I remember from before too - other modules, slightly 
different firmware, all UT+; that's why I checked it again.

>From other mail:

> The Oncore modules themselves have TTL serial I/O.  Everybody
> seems to use a MAX232 or equivalent to convert to RS-232.
> I'm sure one could to USB with e.g. an FT-232, but PPS
> resolution would suffer.

Just confirming all the above, connected to a real serial port for PPS. 
In this case via an MAX3232.  I've also earlier tested with an USB dongle 
(marked TTL-232R-3V3), but no good for timing.  (Even completely lost the 
1 us pulses from sirfstariii module due to USB.)
