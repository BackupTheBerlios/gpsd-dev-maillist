> Here's my proposal for a GPSD URI format:
>
> gpsd://<host>[:<port>]/[<device>][/<protocol>]
>
[...]
> protocol is one of: "raw", "nmea", "gpsd". if unspecified, the gpsd
> protocol will be used.
>
> gpsd://gpsd.mainframe.cx/ - connect to gpsd.mainframe.cx, default
> port, autoselect the receiver, and use gpsd transport (w=1)

There's already a standard similar to what you want to do, called
NTRIP. In fact, GPSD already seems to have an interpreter for it in
/trunk/ntrip.c - and I see you've made commits to it so I assume you
already know the basics. One of the elements of the source table is
the type of data available; it can be (and often is) NTRIP, but it can
also be CMR, RAW, or pretty much anything else the provider wants to
offer.

Why not simply use NTRIP with your three protocol names?
