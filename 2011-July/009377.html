Hello all,
I apologize in advance for any misconduct in netiquette, mailing lists are
"before my time".

Background:
I am working on a project to harness the GPS data from a Palm Pixi Plus for
use on a linux laptop with GSPD. Due to the [proprietary] nature of this
device I am unable to interface with it directly.

However, Palm did include a function to access the data.
The raw output is:
** Message: serviceResponse Handling: 2,
{"errorCode":0,"timestamp":1.311321075963E12,"latitude":-43.6639279127121,"longitude":171.89448022842407,"horizAccuracy":46.04345773288535,"heading":0,"velocity":0,"altitude":-21,"vertAccuracy":12}

I have converted it with BASH to:
[timestamp, latitude, longitude, horizontal accuracy, heading, velocity,
altitude, vertical accuracy]
2011-07-22
01:00:52-07:00, -43.6639279127121, 171.89448022842407, 46.04345773288535, 0,
0, -21, 12

Problem:
Documentation on supported log formats that gpsfake can read is lacking. Web
searches are cluttered with hundreds of mirrors of the manpage.

A sample NMEA log file I came across has this format:
$PGLOR,RID,GLL,182,5,023*4D
$GPGGA,235946.99,,,,,00,00,5.0,,M,0.0001999,M,0.0020199,*57
$GPRMC,235946.99,V,,,,,,,120180,,,N*78
$GPGSV,1,1,01,21,,,46*79
$GPGSA,A,1,,,,,,,,,,,,,11.2,5.0,10.0*36

This page is quite daunting:
http://aprs.gids.nl/nmea/

I've edited the few log files I could obtain and tried to understand the
structure, however trimming the log files and other editing seems to result
in malformed logfiles that the clients do not understand.

Summary:
Is there a way to interface with gpsfake without extraneous NMEA data, just
latitude and longitude?

Any pointers in the right direction of how to convert the data I have into
something gpsfake will properly accept and feed to clients such as xgps and
tangogps would be much appreciated.

Thank you very much,
~ Hazard
