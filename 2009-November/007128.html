Greg Troxel <gdt at ir.bbn.com>:
> The build does not work on NetBSD, due to what I think are unportable
> assumptions about ncurses.

I'm aware of the problem.  But, executive decision: I'm not going to hold the
release for it.  The build works under Linux and OpenBSD; if NetBSD has
to wait for the 2.91 cleanup release, that's how it will be.

The reason I'm not going to hold the release is that autotools is
involved. Even for me, a battle-scarred veteran of the autotools
wars, modifying this kind of test sucks so hard it could pull a
watermelon through nine miles of garden hose.  And tends to
be destabilizing in really annoying ways.

If you can come up with a clean configure.ac patch over the weekend,
I'll merge it.  Maybe.  After eyeing it skeptically.  If you can't,
we'll fix this in 2.91

Someday I'll pound ckuethe into accepting scons and we'll have a a
build system that doesn't have razor-tipped spikes all over it.  Until then,
last-minute changes to configure.ac in the run-up to a major release
will have to be *very* minor to get past me.
