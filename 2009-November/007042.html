On Thu, 12 Nov 2009, Håkan Johansson wrote:

> > Those two checks do different things.  The first is ONLY  the case
> > where the pulse is so short that TIOCMGET does not see it.  If the
> > cycle is off by more than 100uS from 1S then TIOCMGET should have seen
> > it and that test should fail.
>
> No, a short pulse being off by more than 100 us will not make it detectable by
> TIOCMGET.  It's not there when looked for.

But it DOES make it an invalid pulse.

Show me some PPS logs and maybe I'll change my mind.

> > The second check is for the short pulses and for longer pulses that
> > are detectable by TIOCMGET,
> >
> > Neither case has anything to do with 'pull-in'.  If the system clock
> > is so unstable as to not be 1mS accurate from pule to pulse then
> > you should just use that system as a boat anchor.
>
> Why have stricter requirements on the short pulses?

Because if a short pulse is not short then it is not a short pulse.

> Sure.  But a system clock without any reference easily is of by 100 ppm, so
> requiring it to be accurate within 100 us per second prevents it from helping
> before it has been disciplined for a while.

You confuse accurate with repeatable.  I dare you to find a PC clock
that shifts 100pm in 1Sec.  If you find one I'll consider a change.
We have enough repeatable problems to fix than to argue about ones
we have never seen.
