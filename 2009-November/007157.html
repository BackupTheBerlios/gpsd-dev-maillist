On Nov 23, 2009, at 12:58 PM, Eric S. Raymond wrote:

> This patch seems wrong to me.  time_t is definitionally the correct  
> type
> for time in seconds from the Unix epoch.  If the Mac's time_t can't  
> hold
> any result of a calculation that has time in seconds from the Unix  
> epoch
> as a result, then it is the the Mac's definition that is broken, not
> our code.

I don't think that's a fair statement. You can't expect time_t, who's  
size is undefined by the standard, to hold *any* result of a  
calculation that has time in seconds from the UNIX epoch. I think you  
can only expect it to hold a reasonable calculation. Even with an  
unsigned long there will come a time when a particular date will not  
be supported.
