OK, good. However, we should be clear on the goal. Making a good, well-behaved 
Motif app is often at odds with minimizing screen real-estate.

For example, certain widgets (like the satellite info table) might best be 
made to scroll vertically, to allow for even more than 14 satellites. Of 
course, that scrollbar takes real-estate. I know some toolkits can 
conditionally display the scrollbar as needed, but I don't recall if that 
applies to Motif and besides, you need to account for the worst-case size 
anyway.

Another example: Motif 'Frame' widgets around groupings of similar widgets are 
useful for (A) efficient use of the Motif keyboard 'widget traversal' 
mechanism I discussed earlier and (B) making the layout more appealing and 
logical -- but of course, frames take up a little bit of real-estate.

Personally, I think that as long as the default app size is under 800x600 
(which it already is), we shouldn't obsess over real-estate issues too much.

Aside: It's those KDE guys I need to talk to -- they have configuration 
dialogs that are virtually unusable on an 800x600 display! :^)
