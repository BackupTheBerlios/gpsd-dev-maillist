It may be caused by a problem in the TSIP driver (which I wrote), which
may initialize the satellite list in a way that xgps does not expect.
(the use of the receiver channel numbers in gpsd is a bit funny, probably
caused by NMEA roots.  it tries to fill the slots contiguously with valid
satellite information, which is a bit inconvenient when the receiver does
not send all active satellite information in a single message.  this
happens with TSIP)

However, I think an application should not crash when fed incorrect data.
