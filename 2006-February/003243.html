Sorry about that Bill--all you missed was ``here, try this''.

And with the religious war starting, I wasn't itchy to say any
more than necessary.  However, it seems to me that xgps (and
cgps) are intended as simple applications that allow someone who's
decided to try gpsd to

 1) get quick feedback that gpsd and their GPS are working
 2) figure out where, when, what satellites, etc.
 3) (for hackers) get a quick start on writing some application
    that really does what they want.

There clearly are quite a number of ways to write essentially
this same application using different toolkits.  To the extent
that configure can be taught to figure out which ones to try to
build, and to the extent that anyone wants to start writing more
of such things, there is no great harm in adding them to a future
release *except* for the penalty of needing to maintain them.
