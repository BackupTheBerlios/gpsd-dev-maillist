Chris Kuethe wrote:
> On Mon, Apr 11, 2011 at 19:48, Eric Raymond<esr at snark.thyrsus.com>  wrote:
>> Dave has an idea for what he calls the Cosmic Background Radiation Bufferbloat
>> Detector.  His idea is to compare NTP notifications to a high-precision local
>> time reference so he can use the propagation time of NTP packets as a
>> latency measurement.
>
> NTP may be the wrong protocol to use. Or not. You might also want to
> look at IEEE1588 Precision Time Protocol.
>

Not really applicable:

1588 will only work over _very_ local networks, in which case the number 
of routers is likely to be close to zero. I.e. no bloated buffers to detect.

Plain ntpd with stratum 1 clocks (i.e. probably GPS) at both ends of the 
link is much better.
