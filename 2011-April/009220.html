Hi all,

   I am trying to update the openSUSE gpsd package to 2.96. It builds
fine on i586 platforms, but fails on x86_64, the reason being:

> RPM build errors:
>     File not found by glob: /usr/src/packages/BUILDROOT/gpsd-2.96-41.1.x86_64/usr/lib/python2.7/site-packages/gps*.py*

   Those files instead turn out to be in s|/usr/lib/|/usr/lib64/|

   The .spec file responsible for the build can be seen in all its glory at

> https://build.opensuse.org/package/view_file?file=gpsd.spec&package=gpsd&project=home%3Alicehunter%3Abranches%3AApplication%3AGeo&srcmd5=ec4018e07503aad76114d185909f47d8

   The operative parts, however, are:

> %{!?python_sitelib: %global python_sitelib %(%{__python} -c "from distutils.sysconfig import get_python_lib; print(get_python_lib())")}
> %{!?python_sitearch: %global python_sitearch %(%{__python} -c "from distutils.sysconfig import get_python_lib; print(get_python_lib(1))")}

and:

> %{python_sitelib}/gps*.py*
> %{python_sitearch}/gps/

   From the above python command, it appears to expect the files to be
under /usr/lib/, but gpsd installs them under /usr/lib64/ instead.

   Any ideas as to a) who is in the wrong? and b) how to fix? (I'm not
much of a pythonable sort of person) TIA.


Regards,
