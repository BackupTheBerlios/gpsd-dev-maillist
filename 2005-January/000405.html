Petter Reinholdtsen <pere at hungry.com>:
> 
> I noticed these definitions in gps.h:
> 
>   #define METERS_TO_FEET  3.2808399
>   #define METERS_TO_MILES 0.00062137119
>   #define KNOTS_TO_MPH    1.1507794
> 
> Is it smart to have these definitions in the file global header file,
> considering that both feet, miles and knots have varying definitions,
> depending on time and location?

All modern use of the knot follows the ISO standard adopted in 1929;
if you can find me any pre-1929 use of GPS measurements I'll admit there
might be a problem.  :-)

Miles are international miles.   Similarly, the foot is the U.S./British 
imperial foot.  

I think you're being a little over-pedantic here.  The only competing
standard for mile and feet lengths is the U.S. Coast and Geodetic 
Survey one, which even Americans don't use except in direct connection
with USGS maps and wich differs from the international one by less than
three parts in 1000. 

Given that your name looks Scandinavian, I suppose your objection
might be based on the Norwegian and Swedish "mile" of 10 kilometers.
I fear this objection would look silly even to people from Denmark 
or Finland.

I'll add suitable comments to these definitions.

> What are these definitions used for?

KNOTS_TO_MPH is used in xgpsspeed,  The other two are included 
for completeness.
