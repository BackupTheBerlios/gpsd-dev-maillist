Normally I would agree with that, but the mechanism that sirfmon uses to
connect to gpsd isn't at all specific to sirf binary.
So, there is no gpsd support for sirfmon, there is support for super-raw
mode that enables reading the raw data from the receiver, and sirfmon
interprets that.
Anyone could write a tsipmon or rockmon or whatever monitor anytime, and
it could use the same interface.
