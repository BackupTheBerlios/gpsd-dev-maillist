Name: 
"GPSlím 236" - but the picture on the box is lacking the acute accent, and accent usage is mixed by many online vendors 

Compatibility: Good [1]

Chipset: 
SiRFstarIII

Interface: 
Bluetooth native, TTL on mini-USB connector. 
Holux sells TTL->USB (PL2303 [067b:2303]) and TTL->RS232 cables to mate with the unit.
Bluetooth is 9600/8N1, USB with is 38400/8N1.

Vendor: Holux

Works with: 2.33 (didn't test older versions)

NMEA version: V2.2.

Sentences:
GPGGA
GPGSA
GPGSV
GPRMC
GPVTG
GPGLL [2]
SiRF binary [2][3]

Notes:
Be very careful in charging the unit! They ship a wall-wart-style power adapter
that has a USB A socket, and they provide a A-miniB cable for usage with the
adapter. Do NOT use this shipped cable for connecting to the computer - it may
zap the TTL hardware, and your USB stack won't like it either.
I do have the additional USB cable that Holux sells, and it works 99% [4].

[1] All of the basic data EXCEPT altitude are good. Altitude ('a')
	always seems to return zero, despite the GGA sentances coming in
	every second and having the correct altitude. Running xgps, I see it
	only claims to have a fix every 5 seconds or so, and doesn't keep
	the fix data beyond that - for the moment that it does display a
	fix, the altitude is good.
[2] Not enabled by default, you must configure with TTL->USB cable.
[3] SiRF binary does NOT work over BlueTooth.
[4] Other than the altitude, there is one other bit of weirdness I
	noticed while using the USB cable only: I manually ran the hotplug
	add/remove many times while testing, and in one case, gpsd got into
	a weird state that it did not auto-detect the 38400 baud rate
	anymore, and I had to actually restart gpsd for things to work
	again.
