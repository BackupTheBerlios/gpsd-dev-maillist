OK.  I'll forward you what's in my queue, which includes

1) Dave Hylands's sample for his uBlox-based chip, the one that's
currently causing a segfault.

2) A sample for the GPS-360 from Jeff Fisher.

Please do the following:

1. Work with the submitters to get a proper descriptive header put on
   each sample.  The format is described in www/hacking.html.

2. Decide when the sample should be added to the regression tests, 
   and do it when appropriate.

Be aware that the regressions may break when you move between GCC 3.x 
and GCC 4.x -- the problem is drift in the far decimal digits of trig
results due to precision differences between onboard FPUs and software
implementations.  I think a similar symptom can be triggered by moving
bwtween a 32- and 64-bit hardware FPU.

One of my to-do items has been to address this somehow.  It may require
maintaining different check files for different architectures (ugh!) or
desiding those lower-order digits are noise and lowering the precision
of the reporting.
