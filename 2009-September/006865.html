Yep. You've really got me in a quandary now, Mike. Thanks a freaking bunch ;)

The use-case I highlighted could indeed be met by the gpsdrive project - maybe a little surface hacking is needed to customize it, but no biggy.

However, the real issue that was revealed to me by this use-case was the issue of _device transparency_. 'Virtualizing' a device such that its location is transparent to the user-app, is a very unixy thing to do.

The question now is "at what layer does this virtualization take place?"

To me, it seems like a band-aid kludge to put this in the same user-layer as an IM client (conceptually speaking, of course). Especially now that we have the new JSON interface to GPSD, it seems most correct to have a consistent feed of GPS data through this interface to the client app layer, hence placing the 'virtualizing' of devices within the domain of GPSD itself.

Thoughts?
