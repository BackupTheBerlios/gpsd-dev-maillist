Dan Kane wrote:
> There is nothing magical about open source software that prevents you
> from taking a proactive role in the distribution of a project. True, you
> can't really 'forbid' anyone from packaging it, but you certainly can
> request that they stop distributing it until they get it right, and/or
> work with them to fix the problem. If they don't respect your
> intentions, you can make a point of bringing attention to their poor
> standards on the project homepage, if necessary.

Of course - but from my experience it won't help much, as users will always
complain at the distribution first and expect that they pass it on - at least
the 'normal' user works this way.


> Just because a project is open source and 'out there' that doesn't mean
> you can get lazy and be derelict in your care and attention to detail.
> The distributor is almost irrelevant in comparison to the negative
> attention that can be drawn to a misconfigured package.

In my opinion its the other way round - first the people blame the distribution
if something doesn't work as expected, especially when they heard that it is a
"great" programm and they need it.
