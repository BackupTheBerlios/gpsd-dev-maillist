Hi all,

As a follow-up to my earlier message (about interpreting SiRFstar  
binary protocol messages with MessageID 8 -- see below), I can now  
report that I understand what's going on. The SiRF reports raw  
satellite bits in the low 30 bits of the 32-bit words; these still  
need to be subjected to the parity algorithm, which may invert the 24  
data bits (if the lowest bit of the previous word was non-zero).

In case of the SiRF protocol message 8, the lowest-order two bits of  
the previous word are replicated in bits 31 and 30 of the next word,  
in order to facilitate the parameter checking algorithm. This had me  
confused.
