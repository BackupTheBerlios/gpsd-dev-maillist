It's a couple months old, but isn't quite a 'zombie' thread yet ;)

I was trawling the archives before signing up to gpsd-dev, and thought
that Chris Kuethe's idea was interesting.

I had wondered how I could get an 'army' of portable GPS devices (I'm
looking at multiple nokia n810/n900 devices) all hooked up to one
central web portal.

If each device ran a cross-compiled gpsd and 'phoned home' (courtesy of
a client app), then a central web server could run one instance of gpsd
managing multiple feeds from multiple remote gpsd instances, and update
a google map with realtime locations, frex.  Imagine a pizza delivery or
taxi company with drivers so equipped.

Any mileage here?
