Dan,

> The question now is "at what layer does this virtualization
> take place?"

I'm sure it depends on your requirements but most of the tracking systems that I've worked with use a model like this.

  * organizations
  * people and equipment
  * geo-objects (points, tracks, clusters)
  * locations
  * sensors

> To me, it seems like a band-aid kludge to put this in the
> same user-layer as an IM client (conceptually speaking, of
> course). Especially now that we have the new JSON interface
> to GPSD, it seems most correct to have a consistent feed of
> GPS data through this interface to the client app layer,
> hence placing the 'virtualizing' of devices within the
> domain of GPSD itself.

If you are going to drink that water, then you need to look at sensorML and XML to describe the whole process.  

I look at gpsd as a virtualization of consumer GPS devices.  If a company is going to deploy thousands of one device they most likely are not going to use gpsd to virtualize their hardware.
