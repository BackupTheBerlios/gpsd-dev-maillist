Dan Kane wrote:
> I don't share your attitude, Bernd.
> 
> If I have a project, and a distributor is fucking things up, I don't say
> "it ain't my problem, blame them"...I say "hey! distributor! let's get
> this right! my projects reputation is on the line!". If they don't get
> it right, I withdraw my project from their distribution.

Thats not how open source works, although that is sometimes a bit unfortunate.
You can't forbid a distribution (or anybody else) to use your code. If they mess
it up, they're to blame for it.
For example if the gpsd upstream would decide to change the license in a way
that it would Fedora be forbidden to distribute gpsd, this would make the
license non-free according to Debian's DFSG, so I'd move it to non-free in
Debian and stop maintaining gpsd.

> Like it or not, the GPSD project is the paramount stakeholder in its
> future, and sloppy distribution can damage that...we should take great
> care about such details.

Unfortunately all you can do is to open a bug within the distribution, or try to
get in contact with the person who is taking care of the packaging there
(although the right way would be that the distribution contacts upstream, as
they are using upstream's code, and not the other way roud). Or you find a way
to maintain the package on your own, which is a lot of work usually - and
nothing one should expect from an upstream.
