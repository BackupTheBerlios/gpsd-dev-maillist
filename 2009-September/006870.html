Thanks for giving me some good food for thought, Mike, it is
appreciated :)

Obviously, I'm looking at your "sensors" layer and wondering how GPS
devices can be virtualized. GPSD already handles multiple local devices,
so it can be said that there is architectural support for the concept of
a one-to-many relationship between GPSD and devices, respectively. All I
am proposing is that these multiple physical links could be
'virtualized'. Maybe there's no mileage in it, but I think it's worth
considering.

A company deploying thousands of devices is unlikely to be using GPSD,
period, regardless of my proposal. This is no slur on GPSD, but a
recognition that it, and surrounding software, just aren't ready for
that kind of prime-time use. This will change. For now, I'm sure they'll
contract with Verizon or any number of other big-time providers to meet
their needs. In the future, even if they deploy thousands of _one_
device, the advantage of using GPSD is that future changes to deployed
devices (eg. switching manufacturer) should be transparent to
higher-layer software. This may not be the case if they use proprietary
interfaces to manage their devices.

More thanks to you for yet another useful pointer - I had not heard of
SensorML. I'm beginning to get that "can of worms" feeling ;)
