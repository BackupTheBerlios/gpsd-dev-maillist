> I did more logging that shows the differences of calculations coming

I'm glad you are digging into this - it seems very tricky.

@esr: We should be careful about saying "numerically unstable":

  http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Numerical_stability


I understand that we are having a situation where gpsd computes a
different value from the same inputs on different systems, and that the
cause of the difference is trig returning different values for the same
inputs.  But that doesn't mean that the trig is at fault; it could be
that those differences are allowable and that the use of them magnifies
the resulting difference beyond reason - which would be the essence of
being able to label the earth_distance *function* numerically unstable,
not sin/cos.

It would be good to have a regression test for earth_distance.

Michael: How different are the results from sin and cos on various
platforms?
