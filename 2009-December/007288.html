Michael R. Davis wrote:
> GPSD Folks,
> Are any of you working on the geoclue project?  They claim to have an interface to gpsd.  I'd be interested in knowing the difference between their D-BUS service and the stock D-BUS service in gpsd.  Maybe version 3.0 would be a good time to merge the two D-BUS protocols if in fact they are different.  Personally, I like to see a Perl library.
> Mike

I'm maintaining the Debian package of geoclue at the moment...
The main difference of the dbus services is that geoclue is able to get Location
informations from various providers, for example the GSM cell location, and not
only from a gps device.
Unfortunately is upstream not very active, there are a number of bugs open in
their bugtracker and nobody seems to take care of them. I just came up with a
patch to make it compatible with the new libgps, pretty much untested though.
Its listed in the bugtracker on freedesktop.org.

Hope that helps,

Bernd
