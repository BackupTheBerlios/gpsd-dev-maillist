>   sin is sometimes different in the lowest order bit, more or less.
> 
>   For points that are very close, earth_distance computes distances

>   can be several % off (0.25 vs 0.21 or something like that) depending
>   on this tiny difference in sin().

Is it? Consider how far away those satellites are, let's say 10^5
meters. We're talking about a difference of 10^-3 meters or so. That's
an error on the order of 10^-8.

The bit in the sin output is 10^-15, if I remember correctly. Now add in
a couple of numerically tricky calculations (think "two lines crossing
at a very shallow angle" for an example of this sort of problem) and I'm
not sure there's any real problem.
