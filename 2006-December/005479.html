I recently got a Garmin GPS 18 USB as my web searches seem to purport
compatibility with gpsd. The unit itself works fine in Windows but
when I try to use it with gpsd (I've tried 2.28, 2.33, and svn as of
2006-12-27) gpsd will segfault. This seems to happen only when the GPS
unit has enough sattelite locks for a position (i.e. is sending back
positional data). gpsd seems to run fine and respond with no position
while the GPS is "warming up", then segfaults.

Hmmm, this may not be quite true. When running with this command-line:
gpsd -n -N -D 5 /dev/ttyUSB0
I see lots of output and some of the lines seem to give a latitude and
longitude. However, the lat and long numbers are huge, taking about
1.5 times the length of my screen before the decimal point. I've also
seen multiple Lat and Long lines output, Lat and Long output as 0.00
and no output of these at all before the segfault.

When the segfault happens it is always right after a line such as:
gpsd: UTC Time: 1166918386.000000

In fact, it always seems to be that number in my runs just now.

You can view a log of one such run at:
http://pear.reversefold.com/~papercrane/gpsd.log

Any help or pointers would be appreciated.
