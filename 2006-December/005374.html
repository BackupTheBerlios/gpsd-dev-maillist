"Eric S. Raymond" <esr at thyrsus.com> writes:

> 2. Bathymetry.
>
> Carl Brown requested support for the NMEA INDPT sentence.  A test load
> for an NMEA device that emits it has been added to the tests directory, but 
> code for parsing INPDT has not been added.  I agree with the list consensus
> that adding support for this seems a reasonable extension of what GPSD does.
>
> The simple, obvious solution would be to simply report water depth in
> the altitude field, unless we think somebody is going to mount
> bathymeters on airplanes.  Gary Miller observed that there are three
> 'altitudes' at issue:
>
>         altitude of GPS.
>         depth of transducer
>         offset of transducer to water line.
>
> But "offset of transducer to water line" is a fixed offset that, according
> to http://www.humminbird.com/normal.asp?id=853, has to be programmed into
> the bathymetric devices by the customer behind gpsd's back.
>
> I say screw it, gpsd can report the sum of the two as altitude.  This
> avoids opening up a *huge* can of worms about extending the gpsd
> protocol.

The real issue is that adding depth information isn't really about
reporting the position of an antenna, which is what GPS and hence gpsd
do now.  It's reporting a secondary observation at a location.  My
project at work uses GPS to get position of wagons, and we observe
signal strength of beacons from access points (and then share them,
soon to be via software radios with "cognitive control").  So should
we be adding access point signal strength, or barometric pressure, or
temperature?

Another view is that a combined GPS/depthfinder is reporting the
position on the seafloor under a boat, and thus we can use the
altitude field.  But in that case it's a different logical device than
the GPS receiver by itself, and perhaps should be presented as a
separate feed.
