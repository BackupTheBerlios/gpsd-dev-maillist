On Thu, Dec 14, 2006 at 06:30:27PM -0500, Eric S. Raymond wrote:
> I managed to tag the release.  Commits can resume now.
> 
> For the record:  After you ship a release, Chris (or whoever the
> release engineer is),  yo need to do this:
> 
> svn copy trunk tags/release-${VERSION}
> 
> svn commit -m "Tagged for external release ${NEWVERSION}" commit
> 
> This way we have a record in SVN of what we actually shipped.
As a point of reference, there is a way to do it without having the pain
of other commits.

svn copy -r ${REV} \
	trunk tags/release-${VERSION} \
	-m "Tagged for external release ${VERSION}"

Where REV is the SVN revision you want to make the branch with.
