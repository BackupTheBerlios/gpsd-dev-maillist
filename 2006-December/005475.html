On 12/21/06, Ali Utku Selen <selenau at kentkart.com.tr> wrote:
> I worked a few hours on ublox's PUBX extension for NMEA and now have a full
> parser for it. I tried xgps with it and all the fields on screen was looking
> reasonable. But since device emits other NMEA sentences, I have to disable
> them in the code inorder to prevent inconsistency inside gpsd's data fields.

> I'm thinking about seperating the UBX binary and PUBX drivers, we can have
> something like --enable-ubx and --enable-pubx.

No, that's gross. The exact same duplicity of flags exists with
fastrax support (enable-itrax and enable-italk) and is confusing.

In order of preference:
1) use vendor binary if-and-only-if it gives you single-packet PVT
solutions, and doesn't cause more problems. SiRF GSW2 < 2.3.1 is just
such a problematic firmware.
2) use vendor nmea if it gives you a single sentence with a complete
PVT solution. sure, ublox nmea may contain a full fix, but there's so
much more you get from binary mode.
3) suck it up princess, and use generic NMEA.
