> 1. Move to savannah.gnu.org. Use their bugtracker, their mailing list 
> facilities, put the web pages there, etc.
>
> 2. Move the repo and issue tracker to Google Code.  They don't allow
> static web content, so that goes elsewhere (possibly under my hosting
> space at ibiblio.org).  Google Groups sucks, so we host the mailing
> list at librelist.

My take is that you should consider only hosting that is provided by a
501(c)3 nonprofit that has as part of its mission to support
free/open-source software.

If it's true that people have to have a google account and a cookie to
interact with the project on google code, that should be an absolute bar
to that path.

As for savannah, it seems that it's been reasonably reliable (and a
sub-week outage once a year isn't really that big a deal).  It seems
more reliable than kernel.org :-) I know what you mean about FSF having
strong ideology, but I'm not aware of any (hosted, non-GNU) projects
having issues from it.

I am assuming you are not talking about making gpsd a "GNU project", or
copyright assignment.   As I understand it the FSF is ok with providing
hosting to projects that are not "GNU projects", for which copyright is
not assigned, but which are available under a Free license (which gpsd
of course is).
