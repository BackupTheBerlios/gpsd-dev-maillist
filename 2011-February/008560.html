> Me no savvy "L5 tracking".  Explain please?

   The short answer is that in order to keep up with the times (and with
the competition), NAVSTAR is undergoing a series of incremental changes,
amongst which the addition of a new frequency, called L5, carrying an
improved navigation signal and designed for Safety-of-Life
applications--primarily aviation, but available for general civilian use.

   I still don't have neither reliable web access nor any printed
references at hand, but as I recall part of the changes involve a new
and improved navigation message (to be transmitted over L5, possibly
L1/L2 as well?), capable of providing more precise ephemeris data *for
more satellites*, and a 13-bit GPS week representation (known as the
Almanac Reference Week field) which will make rollovers a thing of the
past--and relatively distant future.

   I'll try to dig out proper references in the next couple of weeks,
but in the meanwhile IS-GPS-200 should have some pointers.  Search the
PDF for "L5" and it should direct you to the relevant Interface
Specification document.
