I'm not sure I understand the question?

If the GPS sends a packet with a time stamp in a new second, and the
time is valid, that time is passed on to ntpd and chronyd.  On older
GPS running at 4800bps that time stamp can have a latency of over a
second.  On the SiRF/USB I see 640mSec.

The latency is not really important as that can be compensated for.  The
big problem is the jitter which can be quite large.  Even 100mSec or more.

> > But do you need 10mS or 1mS accuracy?
>
> It's not so much a question of what *we* need as of what GPSD's end users
> need.  I think they'd appreciate as much accuracy as they can get.

I get complaints when people find errors on the order of a few hundred
nSec.  So to some people 1mSec is not even close to acceptable.  I can
only guess at why.
