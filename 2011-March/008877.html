As I'm not in the mood for a flamewar or longish discussion, just a
short reply:

On 03/18/2011 05:30 AM, Eric Raymond wrote:
> Bernd Zeimetz <bernd at bzed.de>:
> Linus Torvalds has figured this out.  The Linux kernel no longer 
> has a "stable" branch, and his logic for abolishing it was much
> the same as mine.

And what happened? a LOT of people complained and we have stable and
long-term maintained kernel releases since some years now. See
http://www.kernel.org
Currently I'm doing the same in Debian. Selecting fixes from master
which fix bugs users reported, cherry-pick them into my patches and ship
that in Debian. Nobody in other distributions does that as it takes a
LOT of time. So Fedora and others just ship the last release with all
its bugs. And no, just releasing faster is not an option as bug fixes
which might be able to go into a stable release a mixed into a lot of
other commits. No way to ship something like that in a stable update.
I'm not asking to slow down, I just want a better release management.
