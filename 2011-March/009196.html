>> "foreign" in this context means don't enforce GNU coding standards.
>
> What does -Wno-gnu do?  No good Google hits.

http://www.gnu.org/software/automake/manual/automake.html#Invoking-Automake

scroll down to -W category and note the allowance for no- prefixing.
But don't expect much, it says approximately the same thing as the
descriptions of the "foreign" option, relating to GNU Coding
Standards.  I don't know if, for example, "-Wall foreign" would be
equivalent to "-Wall -Wno-gnu".   The automake at gnu.org list would be
the appropriate place to ask for clarification.

Incidentally, I failed to mention automake options (as listed in
AM_INIT_AUTOMAKE's argument) can also appear in individual Makefile.am
files.  NTP previously had similar options listed in many individual
Makefile.am files, which I believe have all been removed in favor of a
single set in configure.ac.
