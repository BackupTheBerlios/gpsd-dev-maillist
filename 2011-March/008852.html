Actually, it could, at least most of the time, and probably all of the
time on a fast PC.  The ordinary COM port 8520 UART will interrupt
after 10 bit times give or take a few microseconds, and there is
high-resolution timing (mS or less) available in the Linux kernel and
many other places.  I already timestamp the (end of message) arrival
times when I'm logging NMEA sentences and other data in my version to
millisecond resolution.  At the app level there might be some latency,
but you could do an updated driver that timestamped characters (I have
to do this for another far different application).
