> exemplary one.  "Avoid buffer overrun in xgps.c."  No explanation of

Hm, I'd forgotten that release.  I guess you're right, that qualifies
as a hotfix.

The xgps.c overflow wasn't the security issue, just a potential crash
bug in a test client - xgps.c never ran as root.  (It has since
been replaced by a Python script.)

As you may have seen in CVE-2004-138, the security issue was a pretty
run-of-the-mill syslog(3) format-string vulnerability in the daemon.
There's no evidence it was ever exploited, and in fact the timing of
the privilege drop in gpsd makes it extremely unlikely that it could
have been - the daemon loses root before the first user session socket
is opened.

Thus, in retrospect shipping 2.8 may have been excessively cautious, but
I was trying to be a good citizen and err on the side of caution.
