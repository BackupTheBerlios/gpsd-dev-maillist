Bernd Zeimetz <bernd at bzed.de>:
> to avoid linking and missing header file issues in the future, I'd like
> to replace AC_CHECK_LIB and similar calls in configure.ac by
> PKG_CHECK_MODULES / pkg-config. So the question here is: is pkg-config
> available on all our supported architectures/operating systems?

I don't know, partly because I'm not actually sure what our list of
"supported architectures/operating systems" is.  I mean, I know that
it includes Linux and OpenBSD on both 32- and 64-bit Intel hardware;
I do know we have a Mac port guy so I guess OS/X should be included too.

Beyond that I'm not certain.  I deal with portability issues case-by-case
as they come up.  I think our code is pretty well armored against 
little-endian vs. big-endian issues, and I've observed that our
most serious port problems seem to have to do with numerical instability
in DOP calculation and error modeling.

I think the only non-Intel architecture I personally care about is ARM,
because I want GPSD to run well on smartphones and tablets.  I don't know
what the state of GPSD on ARM is.  I don't recall anyone reporting anything
they described as an architecture-specific ARM failure.

> behind that.

No particular objection.
