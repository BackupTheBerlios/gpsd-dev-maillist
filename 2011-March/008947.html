I guess I should explain my policy about compiler warnings in shipped 
releases.

In short, I won't ship a release that triggers compiler warnings I
don't understand.  We do, on some systems, generate compiler warnings
think I *do* understand - like, on ckuethe's OpenBSD, warnings about
overwritten initializations.  I'll tolerate those when it is not
practical to eliminate them.

I don't understand every optimization that 'volatile' disables.  Therefore
I don't consider it safe to ship code that is in an inconsistent
state about its volatile declarations.
