I have a similar narrow view problem, and was going to ask if "see"
was number in view, used for calculation, etc. but it is irrelevant.

Going to the NMEA 0183 spec, it says GGA is "satellites in view", so
would correspond to the number of entries in GPGSV sentences (note it
also says 12 max, but with the newer WAAS i've seen it larger).  It is
possible manufacturers might do something else, but with the spec, I
can't rely on GPGGA to do what GPGSA does.

This doesn't help when there is only one GPGSA in a second, and the
A/V flag flips in the GLL/RMC in the middle (implying at least 2d) and
most will show altitude even if it switches between 2d and 3d.
