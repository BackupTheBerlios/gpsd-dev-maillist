Michael Ledford <michael at ledford.cc>:
> Some obstruction-free algorithms use a pair of "consistency markers"
  in the data structure. Processes reading the data structure first
  read one consistency marker, then read the relevant data into an
  internal buffer, then read the other marker, and then compare the
  markers. The data is consistent if the two markers are
  identical. Markers may be non-identical when the read is interrupted
  by another process updating the data structure. In such a case, the
  process discards the data in the internal buffer and tries again."
