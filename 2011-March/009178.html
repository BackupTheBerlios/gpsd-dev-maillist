> On 03/29/2011 11:53 AM, Eric Raymond wrote:
>> I'm not nearly as worried about portability to the 486 as I am to ARM,
>> and maybe to a lesser extent MIPS and AVR32.
>
> Why do you prefer ARM? There are various mips devices out there, ranging
> from embedded stuff to routers and to workstations - due to the fact
> that mips(el) CPUs are developed and produced in china these days.

It only matters on multiple CPU architectures where the reader and
writer might not see the memory transfers completed or in order.

Is there a multiple MIPS board and do they have memory barrier instructions?

ARM is only interesting with the most recent dual-core implementations
and the upcoming quad-core, though there are boards with multiple
single-core cpus which would also be affected.

The Xoom and (jailbroken) iPad 2 have multicore ARM processors that
might need this.
