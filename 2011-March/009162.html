Makefile.am had @ in front of a number of commands.  This made trying to
understand what's going on quite difficult.  My own view is that no one
reads make output except to figure out problems, so we should optimize
for that use.  I just committed a patch by Michael Cook that reduces
mystery by removing @.  make check works with it, and results in 27
additional lines of output (make check, after clean); the previous make
check output was 564 lines.  One nice feature is that it's now obvious
how to rerun pieces of the regression tests by hand.
