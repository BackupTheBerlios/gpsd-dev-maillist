Chris Kuethe <chris.kuethe at gmail.com> writes:

> [OpenBSD]
> -x11 (gui, requires X libraries and -main)
> -php (webgui, requires php-gd)
> -main (gpsd, curses clients, libraries)
>
> Only in rare cases do we make a separate -dev package. It has to have
> a relatively large volume of headers and static libraries. 50M of user
> binaries shared libs and 50M of headers and static libs would very
> likely get split. 500KB (gpsd on amd64) isn't worth it.

I agree; pkgsrc tends not to split things unless having them together
causes pain.   Right now there is just gpsd with everything, where
everything leaves out qt and php.

gpsd itself should have a way to build just parts of it, and to build
depending parts assuming the first parts are already installed.  In
pkgsrc split packages are a build-time concept, not just run-time.
(When building on small machines, that point is particularly important.)
