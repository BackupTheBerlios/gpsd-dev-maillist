Remco Treffkorn <remco at rvt.com>:
> A friend of mine is trying tangogps with gpsd. He claims tangogps
> uses the "new" protocol. His gpsd version is 2.39. What is the
> expectation in a case like this? I always wondered how the protocol
> level is negotiated, but never looked...

It's not negotiated; the old protocol didn't issue an identification header.
This is why the changeover was a flag-day event.

For weird coincidental reasins (e.g. the fact that ?WATCH contains a "W", 
the other characters in the command are ignored, and the library recognizes
old-protocol responses) shipping a new-protocol watch to an old daemon
actually triggers responses that the library can parse.
