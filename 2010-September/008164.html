   Just a wild idea, FWIW

On 15/09/10 15:42, Eric Raymond wrote:

> 1. How do we embed timestamp information in test loads?

   Writing the arrival time (or delta time) of each packet, and its
offset in the raw data file, to a separate file?

> 2. How do we deal with the fact that the test framework will feed packets at
> a different speed than they arrived?

   Reading offset2-offset1 bytes from offset1 in the raw data file, then
waiting tstamp2-tstamp1 before sending the next burst?

> ...and *don't* tell me to insert delays in gpsfake to try to mimic the send 
> interval, that would make a regression test far too long a process to do as
> often as we should.

   But, insofar as there might be bugs which might be timing-dependent,
wouldn't it make sense to actually replay the packets at the same, or a
similar, rate as they were originally captured?

   Perhaps the regression tests could be parallelised and/or distributed
to an extent? (it they're not already, not sure)

   As I said, just some random thoughts.
