On 04/14/2010 08:51 PM, Greg Troxel wrote:
> It's rather easy to declare: "We are now in freeze.  All proposed
> commits need to be sent to the list and receive esr approval before
> going in (or someone else's for esr changes!).  Everyone please test."
> Then go at least 72 hours with only measured, clearly-bug-fix changes.
> Lots of other projects do this.

What other projects also do is to create a new branch where the new version can
stabilize while the development in master goes on as before. Bugfixes are
cherry-picked into the release branch then... Same for security/point releases
for older versions - they are just prepared in the branch of that release,
tagged and published.

> A problem is that it feels unmotivating to test when you return to see 5
