Hm. that's odd. If it was just the time that was off (and by
13-15sec), I'd chalk it up to the GPS-UTC leap seconds. But if you're
saying there's some actual serious 10-second delay between the time
the receiver moves and the time GPSD sees that move, there's a
problem.

I say 13-15s because there is about a 1s latency for gpsd to have its
way with your data. and we're currently at 14 leap seconds, and local
clocks are involved too.

My attached screenshot shows a delay in time (i don't think we're
handling leap seconds properly in sirfmon) and a pretty decent match
for the rest of the navigation data. Don't take that to mean I don't
believe you - i'm just going to have to go for a datalogging drive
sometime.
