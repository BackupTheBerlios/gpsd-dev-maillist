Yes, sometimes a GPS will report altitude for a while after it has lost 3D
lock; gpsd doesn't try to second-guess whether this is accurate or not but
simply passes through the report.  

Once the GPS's confidence estimates for the interpolation it is
performing drop below a certain level, it will stop reporting altitude,
and so will gpsd.    The cutoff level varies with GPS and isn't documented
anywhere :-(.
