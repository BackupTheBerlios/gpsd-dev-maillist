On Tue, 26 Sep 2006, Chris Kuethe wrote:

> So everyone likes rekafoobling the energymotron.... I mean,

Prolly best to ask this on gpsd-users.  All the folks in gpsd-dev will
just hack gpsd to do what they want it to do.

Most of the folks that hit-and-run on gps-users just want gpsd to do the
right thing.

So we are left with a narrow range of people that want a handy know.

> Also, a "reverse proxy" mode might be useful - like super-raw, but for
> clients to write to the GPS.

I see this as the answer to getting a knob to turn with out having to
add knobs to gpsd.  gpsd just does the standard thing out of the box.  Then
an adventerous user can use a sirfmon or XXXmon to twiddle his gps.

Another use for a super-raw proxy would be to download breadcrumbs and
such fro ma gps.  gpsd need only pass-thru the download request from a
client like sirfmon and pass the raw data back.
