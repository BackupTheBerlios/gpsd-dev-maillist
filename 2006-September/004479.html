> Other interesting value in this message is 45.271. Is this precission correct?
> Probbably not, why not report just 45.3? It is height of geoid (mean sea
> level) above WGS84 ellipsoid.

Gpsd does compute geoidal separation internally. We do have a built-in
geoid model. I'm inclined to keep it just the way it is, and let the
end user round the figure according to their needs. What if someone
does plug in an ashtech or novatel survey receiver and really is
getting centimetre precision. It's not our place to hide data.

The entire GPS system is predicated on knowing when you can trust
those decimal places. If you calculation your positional error to be
on the order of meters, I wouldn't trust the geoidal separation to
more than meters. If you know (and know why) you have centimetre
accuracy, I'd trust the separation to centimetres.

I won't object much if someone changes the format to "%.1f"
