> When I disable HTM too, gpsd doesn't get any data anymore and gpsd
> doesn't recognize the device anymore (which was actually my first

Unsurprising.  For NMEA to be recognized at all, the packet getter 
has to see a $G or $P packet header with a valid NMEA checksum on
the packet.

Leave $PTNTHTM enabled.  It's the only sentence gpsd recognizes, anyway.

> Maybe we should forward to gpsd-dev to get TNTC working. It should have
> been working once for one user.

Yes.  I recently changed the way the sentence is interpreted so as not to need
a boatload of extra gps_fix structure members. As the comment now says:

     * Heading maps to track.  
     * Pitch maps to climb.  
     * Roll maps to speed.
     * Dip maps to altitude.
 
> Generally I like the way TNTC does hard and softiron compensation and
> I'm interested in my first experiences about that.

I'd like to get this working, and have a regression test for it.
