OK - now I know there is some support for making the "simple" version 
the default behaviour, but obviously we have to take care of the 
"purists" or "experts" too... attached is a patch to "make it so".

I've done a little testing, and will do more just as soon as I figure 
out how my GPS unit that - up until a month or so ago - always 
identified and was handled as NMEA, now identifies and is handled as 
a SiRF-II binary... but essentially, I know from debug output that 
the logic works - now I just want to confirm that I can get the 
jitter with -c set and don't get the jitter without it.  (I swear I 
am not making that up - it has changed, and the only thing I can 
think of is that the Prolific driver update now allows GPSd's 
auto-configure sequence to work when it didn't use to... to my 
knowledge, there has been no substantial change in the auto-configure 
section of the daemon in the past two months).

Hopefully - as long as the "defaults to playing nice" decision isn't 
a deal-breaker - this resolves the last issue I have for a release.
