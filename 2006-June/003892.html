Dan Oetting <dan_oetting at qwest.net>:
> There may be alternate ways to change/track baud rates that are more  
> reliable than assuming you can predict the timing. For instance, when  
> changing the GPS baud rate, send the command to the GPS and wait for  
> the receiver to detect the framing errors indicating that the GPS is  
> transmitting at the new speed before commanding the UART to change.  

As Rob points out in his reply, framing errors are difficult to pick up
reliably in Unix-land.  I think the termios API theoretically supports
detecting them, but some of the relevant features are sufficiently
obscure and little-used that I'd hate to rely on all serial-device
drivers implementing them correctly.

> The current 50ms fixed delay works if only the change speed command  
> can be in the output buffer at the time and the current UART speed is  
> not slower than the default.

Have you actually seen this break?

Your proposed solutions all involve complicating the autobauding logic
a lot.  That makes me nervous; I'd rather stick with techniques that
are simple, stupid, robust, and don't entroduce new feature dependencies
and edge cases.
