FWIW, I own an IOGEAR GUC232A RS-232/USB converter which uses the Prolific 
PL-2303 chip in question.

It advertises "96 Byte buffer for both upstream and downstream data flow" on 
the packaging. IIRC, in practice, it seemed like it was more like 64 bytes, 
but I cannot recall ATM how I came to that conclusion.

And looking at the PDF brochure on the Prolific PL-2303 webpage just now, it 
says essentially the same thing (i.e. 96 byte buffer):

   http://www.prolific.com.tw/eng/files/PL-2303 Market Spec.pdf

   http://www.prolific.com.tw/eng/Products.asp?ID=59

Either way, I think that although 16 bytes is a popular hardware buffer size 
on dongle-type (and maybe GPS-internal) RS-232/USB converters (like my Belkin 
F5U109), larger buffers are not unheard of.
