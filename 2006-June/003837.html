> But (1e6 / 4800) * 10 * 256 = 533333.2480, which is way *longer* than
> the 'safe' delay Til found.  Which, again, causes me to wonder about
> the FIFO-flush-time theory.

I think you need to consider that a buffer of some fixed size (like 256)
does not mean it is guaranteed to contain that much data at the time you
flush it.
Of course, when you do not read for a while and then flush it, it may
always be full (depending on the length of the delay).  But when you
switch speed and then flush it, it could contain an arbitrary amount of
data depending on what the other parts of the detection algorithm do
before the speed switch.
(e.g. if they always read away all pending data before considering a speed
switch, or if the always do so after reading some fixed amount of data and
not recognizing it)
