> I really have no good reason to try to get better than the 7m CEP that
> I have now, but it would be fun to try.  Can you suggest an inexpensive
> solution to get past WAAS accuracy?

That's a tough one. In theory the answer is simple: get a SiRF II based
GPS receiver. That's what I have. However, the good folks at Nokia (who
produce the LD-1W Bluetooth GPS module I've been using) apparently
decided not to connect the chip's DGPS bus to any external interface, or
so it seems. At least I don't get any indication the GPS receiver is
taking the corrections into account in any way. I'm still trying to
decipher the SiRF binary protocol to see if I just need to send some
message to the GPS unit to make it accept DGPS data, but I'm quite
skeptical.

If anyone knows of any 100-200 EUR GPS unit that provably accepts DGPS
corrections, I'd be grateful if you could inform me too.
