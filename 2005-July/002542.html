> I might've bounced along a handwavy description of SS3 earlier,

You didn't.

> but the short version is that the RF/ADC/DSP/FFT/etc that they're running
> inside SS3 runs at about 25x realtime speed. Which means that each
> millisecond they have time to track 20 PRNs and still have time for
> navigation and I/O... The architecture seems to bear a very strong
> resemblance to a software gps receiver with either a wicked fast dsp
> or a hardware fft engine.

I believe it, because that's the way *I'd* design it it.

GPS clock ticks are a whole hundredth of a second apart.  That's eons
of time for a modern embeddable micro.  So why commit to hardware and
freeze your design when software is fast enough?  You keep more
flexibility that way.
