When I said that turning rtcmparityok() into a macro solved that strange
compiler bug I ran into, I spoke too soon.  I have now tripped over an
even weirder phenomenon.  

This build works:

rtcmdecode: rtcmdecode.c rtcm.c rtcm.h
	cc -O2 -fschedule-insns rtcmdecode.c rtcm.c -o rtcmdecode

This build doesn't:

rtcmdecode: rtcmdecode.c rtcm.c rtcm.h
	cc -O2 rtcmdecode.c rtcm.c -o rtcmdecode

That is, for the RTCM builder to compile correctly, -fschedule-insns
must be specified explicitly even though, according to the docs, 
it's a sub-option implied by -O2*!

That is seriously wack.
