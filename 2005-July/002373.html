"Eric S. Raymond" <esr at thyrsus.com> writes:
> The error model gpsd is now using is, at the recommendation of Carl Carter
> from SiRF,
>
> * 8m UERE when DGPS is not available
> * 2m UERE when DGPS is available
>
> To determine whether DGPS corrections are available, I simply go by whether 
> the GPS has its DGPS bit on (the Status field in NMEA).  Would you
> recommend any change in how UERE is computed?

In the absence of any other information from the GPS I don't see a
whole lot of choices.  You could refine it a bit, if the need ever
arises, by scaling up the UERE by the inverse of the signal strength
of the 4 strongest GPS signals.  Thats still not quite right, because
it assumes that those 4 strongest signals dominate the GPS's computed
solution.  That may not be true because they lie in some pathological
orientation.  Sigh, the more I think about it the more it seems like
an impossible task to do a good uere estimate without cooperation from
the gps.
