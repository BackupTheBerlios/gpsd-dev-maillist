I believe that NAV message decoding is a prerequisite to RINEX output
- just a gut feeling, but I seem to recall that RINEX has room for
ionospheric modelling factors, and these are given in the NAV message.

My log database has a bunch of SiRF message-8's (50bps data) in it, so
I think I should try rewrite the decoder. All cool gpsd developers
have a couple of failed 50bps decoders hidden under their beds. :P
RTCM's data format is very similar to that used by the NAV message (on
purpose) so there should be some good reusable code there. And looking
at some other approaches to 50bps data decode, it'll be fugly no
matter who writes it. It's just that hairy.

Good thing I have an old Ultra10 at work: then I can test my code on
ILP32L and ILP64B processors. It's a very effective way to flush out
the endian, sign, and length bugs... grumble grumble db grumble pks
grumble grumble.

CK

message #8. If it's no longer implemented on your receiver, messages
7, 28, 29 and 30 will give you the same information.
