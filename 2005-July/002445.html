Chris Kuethe <chris.kuethe at gmail.com> writes:
> Dunno. But let me just point out that the SiRFstarII has a 12MHz
> ARM7TDMI, and it can compute navigation solutions in C and/or
> assembler pretty quickly. I brought my GPS out to autocross yesterday,
> and while flying around the track, I was burning 6-12% of the CPU.

Does anyone know if SiRF would be willing to provide open-source
compatible details of how to load code into to GPS's cpu?

There are certain things like long-term tracklogs with velocity and
EPE tags that would be great to do in the gps module itself.  Eg. keep
a gps "puck" in your backpack or car along with 3 volts worth of
battery and record 1-second high resolution tracklogs suitable for
map-making.  Current hand-held GPS's only store a very small number of
points and usually forget about recording the signal-quality / EPE and
don't record the velocities at all.

It might even be possible to push some of the gpsd functions into the
GPS itself allowing.
