On Monday 25 July 2005 13:57, Wolfgang S. Rupprecht wrote:
> "Eric S. Raymond" <esr at thyrsus.com> writes:
> > Why does the entire struct has to be reversed?  Why not just shift bits
> > into the current word in the opposite direction?
>
> Well this is certainly a brain teaser and I learned something about
> big-endian bit-fields in the process.
>

IIRC, bit fields are implemented as strings of bytes. As long as we assume 
( don't get me started) a byte to be 8 bits, things are easy.  Endianness 
does not play into it. Think assembly.

Remember, "there is no spoon"!

Bit fields are streams of bits, and as long as there is not a reversed byte 
(and there is not (most of the time)) you don't have to worry about anything.

Bit fields should be immune to endianness problem, unless you assemble them in 
strange ways (like reading anything but bytes at a time).

They sound complicated, but they really aren't.
