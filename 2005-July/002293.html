Chris Kuethe <chris.kuethe at gmail.com>:
> If I understand, you're proposing to make gpsd listen on the usual
> port for the usual clients, and then also listen on 2101 an be a dgps
> (waas) beacon server?

Yes.  But Wolfgang has raised some hard questions about whether this
would actually be useful.  

Assuming it is, consider this possibility...

RTCM is just another packet type.  Clients are accepted on port 2101
if gpsd has recognized any devices that emit RTCM packets.  A client
connection on port 2101 sees only RTCM devices and is automatically
put in super-raw mode.  A client on port 2947  sees only non-RTCM 
devices.  When any RTCM devices are open, their data is automatically
echoed to all local GPSes.

Down the road, the trivial RTCM driver (grab a binary packet, send 
up a binary packet) could be extended to generate useful reports.
