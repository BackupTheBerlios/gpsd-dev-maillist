> > inside SS3 runs at about 25x realtime speed. Which means that each
> > millisecond they have time to track 20 PRNs and still have time for
> > navigation and I/O... The architecture seems to bear a very strong
> > resemblance to a software gps receiver with either a wicked fast dsp
> > or a hardware fft engine.

Nothing wrong with that.

> GPS clock ticks are a whole hundredth of a second apart.  That's eons
> of time for a modern embeddable micro.  So why commit to hardware and
> freeze your design when software is fast enough?  You keep more
> flexibility that way.

Thousandth of a second, but who's counting? ;)

There's a lot of really cool research being done in software gps
receivers, hardware is stupidly cheap and powerful these days, so it
makes a lot of sense to just do it in software instead of making a
custom mask. And flexibility is very much a concern as Galileo is
looking like a not-too-distant reality. I keep hearing that Galileo's
Signal-In-Space is going to be NAVSTAR compatible, so hopefully all
it'll take is a new firmware and all of our receivers can now play
with Galileo too.
