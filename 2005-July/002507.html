Rob Janssen <gpsd at xs4all.nl>:
> > You suggest alternative strategies of (a) sampling more packets before
> > locking on to a packet type, or (b) allowing the driver to kick the
> > packet getter back to ground state.  I'm resisting (a) because it
> > would increase first-fix latency, and (b) because of the design
> > heuristic that single things should be done in a single place. I think
> > it's the packet-getter's job to validate packets -- having some tests
> > in the driver itself could be a maintainance headache down the road.
> 
> On the other hand, this strategy could eventually paint you in the corner
> when even more protocols surface that use the DLE-stuffing packet layer
> (quite common for serial protocols) but have different payload structures.

True.  This is a real risk of the strategy, and I can see it as clearly 
as you do.

On the other hand, I write compilers and compiler-like translation
tools for fun.  (For a recent example, see doclifter, the tool I wrote
to lift [nt]roff markup to XML-DocBook -- compared to that monster the
gpsd packet getter is trivial.)  That is the kind of problem we're
actually looking at here, disambiguating input using lexer/parser like
techniques, and I'm quite good at it.  

Therefore I am confident that if anyone can keep us out of that corner, I can.

If it becomes impossible to disambiguate without driver-level checks,
we can put in a packet-getter hook to reset the machine to ground
state, or some preferred state dispatched on the packet type.  But
we're not there yet.
