You're right, the dgpsip client is almost completely redundant with what
gpsd already does.  The one part of it worth keeping around is the RTCM
decoding; I think I'd like to carry a stripped-to-the-running gears version
that just does your -d function, and maybe the satellite-view logging.  The
point would be to have a program the user can run against a host to check
that it's emitting good RTCM104 and optionally dump it in a readable form.
 
> That server shouldn't be doing anything surprising.  It just has your
> standard poll loop looking for input from the DGPS radio and sending
> it to all the TCP sockets that can accept data.  It is just your
> standard user-mode program.  

I'm somewhat tempted to fold this into gpsd -- that is, give it a second
serial device class that is "DGPS radio" and allow clients to open 
connections on port 2101.  All the logic for managing multiple client
sessions would be identical -- in fact. I'll bet I could add this
feature in less than a hundred lines of code.
