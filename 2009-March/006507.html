Not to forget that handling library transitions is nothing special, especially
not for a developer who will rebuild his program every other day (at least
that's what I assume, maybe it is different in navit...). The normal user is not
affected by such transitions anyway, as every good distribution will take care
of the transition. So even if libgps would bump its soname every other release,
rarely anybody would notice. As it is possible to install several versions of a
library with different sonames at the same time, I even more can't see why
Martin complains about bumping the soname. So in my opinion the complaint about
too many needed soname bumps is just b***sh**^W not valid.
