On Thursday 19 March 2009, Michael R. Davis wrote:
> > If we have a preference how the daemon should be started, we should
> > communicate that.
>
> I think we have.
>
> RPM
> PIDFILE=/var/run/gpsd.pid
> CONTROL_SOCKET=/var/run/gpsd.sock
> OPTIONS="-n -F $CONTROL_SOCKET -P $PIDFILE"
>
> PKG
> DAEMON_OPTS="-F /var/run/gpsd.sock"
>
> HOTPLUG
> CONTROL_SOCKET = "/var/run/gpsd.sock"
> gpsdcmd = "gpsd -F " + CONTROL_SOCKET
>
> > Fedora has local policy (settings) in /etc/sysconfig
>
> The RPM does not currently support this but it could.
>
> Mike

Right. Seeing this, I suggest we restore -n to what it used to be, and deal 
with what we think should be the "right way" of running gpsd on this level.

People who start their own daemon can be expected to have read the man page.

Now it boils down to -n, or not -n.

My personal preference would be -n.

But I really have no strong feelings either way.
