Hello folks

Following submission of a couple of bug reports on the gpsd bug tracker Eric
asked me if I would like to be more involved with the dev group, which I am
happy to do, so this is to say 'hello' and to let you know some more details
of the issues I have come across. In addition I have attached my suggested
fixes for these for consideration by those who will have a broader picture
than I have at this point.

I should say that I am not able to give a large time commitment to the dev
team but am happy to help in regard to the issues that affect my use of gpsd
and areas where I may develop some knowledge through this. At the moment I
am concentrating of the use of gpsd for timing derivation although in a
while I may need to use more of the positioning functionality.

I have just submitted another bug report via the tracker and the details of
this and the others can be found there:

1. 	PPS NTP interface sets shared memory time stamps incorrectly
https://developer.berlios.de/bugs/?func=detailbug&bug_id=15297&group_id=2116

2. 	ntp pps lock unreliable for certain gps unit latencies
https://developer.berlios.de/bugs/?func=detailbug&bug_id=15298&group_id=2116

3. 	NTP PPS interface does not use interlocks correctly
https://developer.berlios.de/bugs/?func=detailbug&bug_id=15348&group_id=2116

These bugs all lead to non-locking or unreliable locking of ntp to gps time
as delivered by gpsd.

I attach tweaked (from 2.38) versions of  ntpshm and libgpsd_core with my
fixes for these issues


Tim

If your interested to know who I am and the kind of thing I'm involved with
see www.telensa.com and www.plextek.co.uk
