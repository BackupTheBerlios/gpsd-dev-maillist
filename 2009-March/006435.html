Greg Troxel <gdt at ir.bbn.com>:
> I read that a while ago, but I'm coming at this from the user point of
> view: the externally-observable behavior is wrong.  I see the point of
> the design guidelines, but I think correctness is more important.

What's "correctness" in this context?  Merging reports or minimizing
letency?  Different users may have differing priorities, so I don't 
want to set either choice in stone at the GPSD level.

> Avoiding malloc isn't intrinsically righteous - it's a strategy to avoid
> bugs.

True, but as far as I can tell irrelevant to what you're objecting to.

> It may be that since cgps is using the non-raw NMEA that it should only
> pay attention to the better sentence.

"Better" sentence is not well-defined here.  GGA gives altitude when available,
but its timestamp is even more mutilated than RMC's.  And some devices don't
emit GGA.

>                     And it's also possible that the
> gpsd-to-client protocol shouldn't be NMEA.

Um...I'm confused.  When it is it *ever* NMEA?

> This may be just a cgps/xpgs issue; callbacks providing fix structures
> with different components valid is more reasoable than a UI showing '2D'
> erroneously and removing the altitude from the display.

At any given time, the display is correct for the last incoming
sentence.  The 2D is not erroneous, it means the last sentence you saw
was RMC.  You can change this behavior with -j 1,
