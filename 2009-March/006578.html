The request/response protocol for AIS has already been defined by the US Navy in a Maritime Domain Awareness Community Of Interest (MDA COI).  Here's an XML representation I found on the Internet.

http://sine.cim3.net/file/pub/MDA%20DS%20COI%20Pilot%2012%20Oct%20-%20COI%20Forum%20Presentation.ppt#513,22,XML Schema Definition & Instances

I don't think gpsd should provide this service necessarily but it could provide all of the data so that an XML service could easily layer on top of GPSD.  

It won't be long until we will have a location service protocol akin to or as part of Universal Plug and Play (UPnP).  More and more mobile devices need geolocation to provide geo specific services.  Why not have that location service be provided by gpsd over the lan?  All we would have to add is a discovery service.
