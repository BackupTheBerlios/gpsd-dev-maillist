"Eric S. Raymond" <esr at thyrsus.com> writes:

> Lukasz Stelmach <stlman at poczta.fm>:
>> There are several ways to configure a programme:
>> 
>> 1. Compiletime - there could be a configure option that determines
>> the hardcoded default and the command lines available (and visible
>> in the help message).
>
> No.  We'll pick the *right* default, once we figure out what it is.
> Much as I love the Unix tradition, I will not succumb to the Unix
> disease of lazily avoiding design decisions with excessive
> configurability.

The problem is that there are two classes of use cases.  One cares about
battery, and the other about being running right away.  You can't pick a
single right default.  Avoiding configuration when it's avoidable makes
sense, but avoiding it when it isn't leads to things like writing a gpsd
client that just opens and listens and starting that at boot to force
opening.
