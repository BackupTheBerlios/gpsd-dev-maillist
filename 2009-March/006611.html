Yo Eric!

On Thu, 19 Mar 2009, Eric S. Raymond wrote:

> Gary E. Miller <gem at rellim.com>:
> > If I am remember correctly it was the 10,000th time a new user
> > complained that when he connected to a long running gpsd that they
> > did not get a valid fix for 2 minutes.
>
> Well, that's at least *an* argument.  We have to trade that off against
> the battery-drain issue, though.  What's worse, delaying first fix while
> the GPS warms up, or silently draining the user's batteries the entire
> time the GPS is plugged in?

What's worse depends on the user.  The interesting thing is the relative
numbers of users that care about battery life versus those that care
about time to first fix.

Given the ratio of complaints to the mailing I think battery life is
the issue to fewer people.  But  certainly no less important to them.

> That's not a rhetorical question either (he said, ecoing Remco).  My
> *instinct* is to be conservative and say we should not be draining the
> user's batteries.  But without better information on how serious the
> battery drain is, I don't know how to evaluate this tradeoff more
> rationally.

So we give the user both options, and flip a coin (or use a config option)
to decide the default.

> > Cut the baby in half and add options to do both, with the default to
> > keeping the GPS alive.
>
> Um, we only need one option - reverse the default.

Can't get rid of the old -n for legacy reasons.

If we keep the default to prefer always on ( time to first fix) then we
need an option for the old default (battery saving).
