> > Right.  My only reservation is that I'd like to hear actual data about
> > the seriousness of the battery-drain problem beforeb I develop my own
> > final conclusion on the matter.
> > 
> Last time I tried this with my Qstarz receiver, logging mode lasted 35h 
> while receiver mode (w/ Bluetooth) died after 15h or so.

That's a significant hit - dwell time cut by more than 50%!  Worse
than I was expecting, actually, and a pretty heavy burden for anyone
who advocates defaulting to no-wait.

> The flip side, however, is that reconnecting to the thing takes a long
> time and doesn't always succeed, particularly just after disconnecting.
> In other words, restarting the client usually doesn't work and requires
> multiple retries.
>
> Therefore, I recommend to default to always-on.

I'm not sure how that recommendation follows, becausr your account
does not establish what is actually going on.

The "takes a long time" could be the known problem with fix delay after
warm-booting the device.  What kind of "long time" are we talking,
here?  2 minutes?  15 minutes?

But the more pressing question is what you mean by "doesn't always
succeed"?  Do you mean that the daemon completely fails to achieve
packet sync on the device?

Hm.  I think I'm going to have to change the default in trunk just so
Lukasz can test in wait mode on his receiver.  This decision is not
final, but we need to get a better handle on what the failures are in
both modes.
