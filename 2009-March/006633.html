Matthias Urlichs <smurf at smurf.noris.de>:
> Eric S. Raymond:
> > But the more pressing question is what you mean by "doesn't always
> > succeed"?  Do you mean that the daemon completely fails to achieve
> > packet sync on the device?
> > 
> It fails to achieve a serial link. The reason for that is that the
> GPS receiver, to save on battery, doesn't keep its Bluetooth subsystem
> turned on all the time when there's no connection, thus a connect
> attempt may time out and fail.
> 
> This causes /dev/rfcomm0 to return I/O errors, which causes gpsd to 
> not retry opening this device, which is, frankly, a lot more annoying
> and disruptive than any additional drain on the battery.

Noted.  That is a serious downside.

What kind of I/O errors?  It sounds as though gpsd should perhaps *not*
give up on just any I/O error, but retry periodically some number of times 
or over some number of seconds.

How does the Bluetooth firmware know to turn back on?

It sounds like we actually have a separate issue here that needs to be
addressed somehow.

We're also still in coin-flip territory.  You're a Blutooth user who wants 
nowait, Lukasz Stelmach is a Bluetooth user who wants wait behavior.  What's
a poor project lead to do?
