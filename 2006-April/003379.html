Hello,

I noticed you can register to the EUREF-IP Pilot Project at 
http://igs.ifag.de/index_ntrip_reg.htm to gain free access to several 
Ntrip streams from around the world.

After spending hours trying to find dgps-ip servers on the net, it seems 
to me that there are a lot more Ntrip streams available on this one 
broadcaster than there are public dgps-ip servers out there. I'd 
therefore like to add at least partial Ntrip support to gpsd.

I know the standard isn't open, but the partial documentation and C and 
Perl example implementations on the site give quite a good picture of 
the protocol basics.

The real issue for me is whether the gpsd RTCM decoder is able to handle 
the Ntrip streams or not. I'm mot familiar with the RTCM protocol, but 
appears that version 3.0 isn't at least backwards compatible with 2.x.

How is it with different 2.x versions then? I'd assume a RTCM 2.3 
listener is able to understand a RCTM 2.0 stream, but would a 2.0 
listener be able to get anything at all from a 2.3 stream?
So which RTCM version(s) does gpsd currently support?

Best regards,
Ville Nuorvala
