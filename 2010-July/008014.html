On Mon, 5 Jul 2010, Bernd Zeimetz wrote:

> normally I'd discuss such changes before pushing them, but I've accidentally hit
> the push button with that change included: Its enhancing xgps to use cairo to
> draw the skyview, which is a bit nicer to look at then the gtk skyview.
> 
> In case somebody doesn't like cairo or it breaks something, I don't mind to
> revert it.

I messed about with this this evening and the results are here:

http://www.discontinuity.info/~rowan/xgps-cairo/

The first of those patches is not necessary as I thought, because 
gtk's CairoContext actually does have a set_color() method, whereas 
pycairo's does not.  Oh well, at least it brings the rendering in line 
with pycairo.  The other patches play with grid fitting the circles 
and lines to make them sharper, a bit of house work, and making the 
rendering resemble the gdk version's look and feel (only antialiased.)
