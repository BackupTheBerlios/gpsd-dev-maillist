On Thu, 1 Jul 2010, Greg Troxel wrote:

> No, what I do is to make a single small change to master, and try it out
> locally, and then just push.  Sometimes I have to pull and rebase (to
> lose the merge commit) because someone else pushed in the interim.

You can safely avoid merge commits using "git fetch" instead of "git 
pull" before doing the rebase.  The difference is that pull = fetch + 
merge, but you actually don't want to merge upstream on top of your 
work, but the other way around.  Instead, by first updating 
origin/master with "git fetch" and only then doing the "git rebase 
origin/master", you're safely rebasing your local commits on top of 
the upstream rather than first merging upstream on top of your work 
and then rebasing.
