Ed W <lists at wildgooses.com>:
> In general the best practice with git is to make use of it's
> powerful branching to effectively use branches as a way to "stash
> patches" and give those patches a name

I'm aware of this norm, but I haven't been pushing it for gpsd because
we have a different philosophy here.

The trouble with being branch-intensive is that it reduces the amount of 
testing that your development head will get.  In extreme cases it can be
difficult for testers to even know what the right branch is.

What I encourage instead is lots of small incremental changes to master
with each being checked through our regresssion-test suite before commit.
While this occasionally has disadvantages it does ensure that, usually,
everyone is looking at the same code.
