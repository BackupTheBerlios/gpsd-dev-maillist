We have discussed it a bit before. Short version, this would be super
shiny to have, but it'll be icky to do.

Also this was predicated on a new protocol (gpsdNG) that is actually
expressive enough to carry this kind of data. The old, single-letter
namespace had been mostly exhausted. Our new JSON format is extensible
to ... whatever. Somewhere down the line we'd like to do raw
measurements out.

We have protocol references for many devices that support some form of
assistance but none of them have a standard format. Some devices want
a broken-out structure, others want the raw 50bps data stream... A few
devices do have a way to blacklist certain PRNs but I wouldn't count
on that, and given time and a fresh ephermeris/almanac, is it worth
blacklisting one?
