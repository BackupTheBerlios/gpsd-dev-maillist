> Timo, why do you want ton xport log levels?  They're a convention internal
> to the daeomon., notb the client library, and I'd rather keep them that way
> for modularity reasons.

I tried to explained the reasoning in the bug report at
https://developer.berlios.de/bugs/?func=detailbug&bug_id=17659&group_id=2116
which reads:


In foxtrotgps

https://bugs.launchpad.net/foxtrotgps/+bug/610743

shows how periodic gps_open() attempts spam ~/.xsession-errors. It
seems that gps_open calls gps_open_r which calls netlib_connectsock
which calls gpsd_report of gpsd_report.c and that writes
unconditionally to stderr.

The file has a comment

/* stub logger for clients that don't supply one */

Does this mean that foxtrotgps could supply its own gpsd_report
function? If yes, then I think LOG_SPIN should be in gps.h so that
clients could refer to log levels by their symbolic names.
