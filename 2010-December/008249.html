Randy wrote:
> Hi all,
>
> Must MMSI be a 9-digit number such as 412000000?Could it be 8-digit or
> even 0?

A ship or coast based MMSI must be 9 digits.  If you have an 8 digit number then it is probably 
missing the leading digit which is the geographical identity, which is one of the following:

     * 2 Europe
     * 3 North and Central America and Caribbean
     * 4 Asia
     * 5 Oceania
     * 6 Africa
     * 7 South America

It is an unfortunate regional practice in the USA for USA registered vessels travelling solely 
in US waters to omit the leading "3" from their MMSI.  This is apparently because the USA is 
the only country in the world.  However in that case you'd mostly get an 8 digit number of the 
form "669xxxxx".  You can safely pretend that there's a leading "3" in those cases.

There is a special case which are seven digit MMSIs beginning with the digit "1" (no 9 digit 
MMSI begins with a 1, all MMSIs that begin with a 1 should be 7 digits).  They are Inmarsat A 
stations transmitting over FM.  I was under the understanding that they had all been 
discontinued in 2007, but I may be mistaken.  It's also my understanding that they transmitted 
on a band that most AIS receivers wouldn't detect, however someone may have hacked one to 
transmit on the normal AIS band, and/or I may be mistaken.

I think it's bogus.  "1100000" as a 7 digit MMSI would have been a valid Inmarsat A terrestrial 
or satellite service prior to 2007.  If it's a satellite service then I'd like to know what it 
reported as lat/long/speed.  In any case I'd like to know what it reported as position and 
vector because no terrestrial Inmarsat A service ever transmitted over AIS as far as I'm aware. 
  It could be that there's something bogus floating around out there that's incorrectly 
transmitting an obsolete 7 digit Inmarsat A MMSI with a bogus zero appended to the end, and if 
that's the case then the appropriate maritime authorities should be notified.
