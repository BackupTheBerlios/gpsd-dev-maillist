The point is the opposite :-)  When running several GPS modules attached 
to the same machine, they will generally issue the PPS signals at the same 
time (top of the second), which makes it hard for the computer to 
time-stamp both with precision.  Therefore, the gpsctl option to be able 
to ask one of them to shift the pulse.  Then the automatic correction in 
gpsd to not have to enter the offset manually in ntpd or chrony. 
(Helpful in the cases when the actual SHM segment used varies during 
testing.  And avoiding the invariable error in sign.)
