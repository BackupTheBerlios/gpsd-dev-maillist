Chris Kuethe writes:
 > i think he just dislikes xml... as i do. the "who's yer daddy" is an
 > amusing expression of frustration at the modern fad of xml-izing
 > everything, even things that shouldn't be.

Yep.  xml is not a Unixy data format.  Unix programs are REALLLLLY
good at handling newline-delimited data.  XML data can be written
without a single newline.

You can losslessly render any XML file in the data format I
recommended.  When you do, you'll have something that you can sensibly
run through a Unix filter.

That said, Unix programs understand lines very well, but they don't
understand paragraphs very well.  You can define paragraphs two ways:
newline-terminated/separated (as you wish) or tab-indented (which is
the style I recommended).

yet, so not much shows through into GPX.
