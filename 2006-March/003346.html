Hello gpsd developers

I think this is an excellent project that really serves the GPS community as a 
whole. I think more people need to know about it--academics and receiver 
manufacturers as well as standard users.

Those of you who are active in GPS research and product development have 
probably heard about the Institute of Navigation, or ION, and in particular is 
Satellite Division. If you haven't then, then here is the practical upshot: the 
ION satellite division contains the largest organized group of GPS developers. 
The entire span of GPS users is represented -- from academics to military to 
commercial vendors. If you want to read up on the ION and its subgroups and its 
missions go to www.ion.org.

I think the ION satellite division needs to hear about gpsd but I am certainly 
not qualified to speak to the topic. That is why I am posting this to your 
developers list. The largest ION conference is coming this September, and, for 
the first time, there is an Open Source session! Our project, the GPS Toolkit 
(GPSTk) has already submitted an abstract. Although the abstract submission 
deadline is over, the chair of the session has notified us (see attached) that 
there is still room in the Open Source session.

I apologize for the spam. Please consider participating. I think this is a 
groundbreaking event for the GPS community.

Regards,
Ben Harris
