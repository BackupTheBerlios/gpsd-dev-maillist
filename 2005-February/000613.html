Gary E. Miller <gem at rellim.com>:
> Having to use garminusb2nmea to interface a Garmin GPS 18/USB to gpsd
> does not seem like a good long term solution to me.

I agree.
 
> 1. What is the best way to present data to gpsd internally?  One way would
> be to convert the Garmin private to NMEA strings and pass it along
> internally to gpsd.  Another way would be to byass the NMEA stage and
> write directly to the gpsd internal data structures.
>
> 2. What is the best file structure to integrate the code into gpsd?  I
> would think a separate file for the Garmin stuff would be the way to go.
> Sort of like zodiac.c.

I'd say the correct answer to both questions is to emulate the way zodiac.c
works now.  It passes data direct to the internal structure, but fills the
internal buffer accessible to the raw hook with an NMEA translation of the
binary data.
