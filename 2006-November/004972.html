I've decided that every chipset/protocol that's moderately nice to
hack on has something about it that truly sucks.

tsip: no length in header, byte-stuffed DLE-delimited.
sirf: no processor cycle counter and packet intervals aren't always reliable
italk: length field sometimes lies
garmin: length field sometimes lies, garmin aren't very nice about documentation


italk is a protocol, iSuite is the software, itrax is the hardware
platform. itrax looks like it can use uNav, Sony or SiRF processors...
if anything it's the iSuite software that's busted.
