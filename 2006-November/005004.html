> Once upon a time I muttered something about old SiRF chips responding
> to break... I think this would be a good time to try. Protocol
> switching via NMEA was something that was added later.

You did.  Trouble is, the Sandpiper spec appears to claim that the conventional
probe and switch sequences we know from SiRF II and III ought to work.  Of
course this could be an error wither in the composition or transmission of
the Sandpiper docs -- do you have a SirFStar I orotocol reference we can check?

Besides -- if break really slams the device to 38400 at 8N1 binary, its 
utility as a probe is kind of limited.  It would have been fine when
gpsd was all we were worrying about and stepping on device settings 
didn't matter.  But we're trying to be kinder and gentler now; in 
particular, gpsctl wants non-invasive probes.
