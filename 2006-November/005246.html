On 11/14/06, Frank Nicholas <frank at nicholasfamilycentral.com> wrote:
> I assumed gpsd ONLY reverts the changed it made.  What if I want to
> leave my GPS in the state, by coincidence, that gpsd set it in (maybe
> from NMEA to SiRF)?
>
> Wasn't that one of the uses of the former gpsctl tools?  To change
> modes of the GPS?

gpsd (the daemon) tries to fully ascertain the device's configuration
at startup. If some configuration changes are necessary, they are
done. When the daemon exits, gpsd (the daemon) tries to undo all of
its changes.

GPSD (the package of useful gps utilities) contains a program called
gpsctl for specifically commanding your receiver to a particular
configuration. We just killed off the fooconfig, barctl, wooftune and
mumbletool programs. It was in no-one's interest to have separate
utilities for each device when a) the operations they perform are
needed by gpsd and b) the code was redundant.
