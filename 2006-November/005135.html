As far as the Motif issue, I'm a long-time (14 years, on-and-off) Motif 
user/programmer and like the toolkit a lot, especially since its 
documentation seems to run rings around Gtk (and especially Gtk2). However, 
it seems that "the writing is on the wall" -- even Slackware (my beloved 
distribution of many years) has silently dropped the OpenMotif package in the 
latest Slackware 11.0 6-CD release. Yes, I know about Lesstif (I was writing 
Motif apps in SunOS and eventually GNU/Linux since before most [including 
myself] had ever heard of Lesstif -- any of you remember "Moo-Tiff", the $100 
[ouch!] Motif clone for GNU/Linux?) And although I still maintain several 
Motif-based apps, I haven't written any _new_ Motif apps in many years now. 
In my experience, Motif got a very bad (undeserved) reputation from _users_ 
because the defaults were very ugly -- something that was easily changeable 
via the "app-defaults" settings. But I've rarely heard Motif _programmers_ 
complain too loudly about the toolkit.

Having said all that, I'm going to start a new thread to describe the new 
Gtk2-based 'xgps' in detail. I've also got a few screenshots so that you can 
see what I've been thinking about.

Bill Marr
