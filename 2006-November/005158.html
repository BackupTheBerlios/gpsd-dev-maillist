I'm always amused at apps which display a too-large, space-wasting 
satellite-shaped icon on their geometry display (with PRN nearby, but outside 
the graphic). To me that seems to favor form over function.

I'm a pragmatist and I tend to like the circles enclosing the graphics, for 
their lack of ambiguity (about what text goes with what satellite; not a big 
issue, I'll admit) and the waste of the space. My big circles look very 
un-satellite-like, but they're unambiguous and don't waste space.

That said, I'm willing to change the app to use your suggestion if that is the 
consensus (and/or if you feel strongly enough about it to veto any 
contradicting consensus :^} ). In other words, it's not a "show-stopper" 
issue for me.

P.S. Although your expansive non-tech vocabulary has occasionally sent me 
scrambling for the dictionary, this tech term ("Roche limit") is the first 
time, IIRC, that your tech vocabulary has sent me scrambling to Google. 
Consider myself educated. :^)

Thanks for your input -- much appreciated.
