I have a better handle on the problem(s) now,  In general, gpsctrl
is reliable at auto-syncing to SiRF binary and switching it to NMEA.  Some
residual flakiness there seemed to vanish when I added a 300-millisecond 
delay after the control operations before closing the device.  

I had no specific reason for this, understand -- it was just that we've had 
so many timing-dependent problems that went away when delays were inserted
after parameter-set operations.

The other side, detecting SiRF NMEA and switching it to binary, is still
chancy. The switching part isn't the problem, it's the autodetection --
sometimes, the SiRF chip just never seems to respond to the "$PSRF105,1"
probe at all.

You muttered something about firing breaks at a balky devices.  I'm
wondering if adding a tcsendbeak() at the start of the hunt sequence
would be a good idea.  Wha does this actually do to SiRFs, in your
experience?
