  cgps is that way for two reasons:

1.  It's the only digital compass I know about that we support.
2.  It's the only way I know to detect it with the existing gpsd.

  That being said, if gpsd were extended in a way to support more
general devices (such as GPS, COMPASS, DEPTH SOUNDER, LORAN, etc) and
report them to clients, it would simple to restructure cgps appropriately.
