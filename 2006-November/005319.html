Jeff Francis <jeff at gritch.org>:
>                                   A formal bugs list would allow us to 
> keep things in a place where they won't fall through the cracks, plus it 
> would be a place where users could look to see if something is a known 
> issue.

I think we already have such a thing.  It's the TODO file.  Chris and I are
pretty careful about keeping it in sync with current reality.

I'm not opposed to using Bugzilla or some other issue tracker, but I'm
also not sure the complexity overhead of doing so is justified yet.
What do we need that Bugzilla does give us and a version-controlled 
TODO file does not?

This is in no way an attempt to squash your suggestion, You may well
be right that our present, less formal mechanisms aren't doing the
job. But if so I want to understand why that is before moving.
