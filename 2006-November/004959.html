On 11/6/06, Eric S. Raymond <esr at thyrsus.com> wrote:
> I think you said you've given up on iTrax support.  Does this
> mean itraxtool can go away too?

Well, I suppose itraxtool can be removed from the repository, but I
haven't given up on it. I am hacking at fully supporting the protocol,
but it's taking some doing to
a) get the checksum right,
b) find some way to handle "short" packets - ones whose length on the
wire differs significantly from transmitted length, and
c) double-check all of my work against usb-bus and serial dumps from
the windows tools.

These runts are messing up the packet getter - if the length is
believed, we land in the middle of another packet, and if we just try
reading until we find a valid checksum followed immediately by '>' ...
well that's a lot of work. And it's predicated on having a working
checksum routine.

I guess I need to go for a drive, sit in my heated leather seats and
get about 15 minutes of real, good, useful data. I have that for SiRF
(hopefully for TSIP soon) but not yet for iTalk.
