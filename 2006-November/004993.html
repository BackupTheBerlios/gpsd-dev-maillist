Now *that* is interesting -- because I have a couple of power supplies from 
old dead Linksys boxes in my basement, saved against just such a day as this.  

Rummage, rummage...ah, here we are: a little ol' wall wart that says on
it "DC 5.0V 2.5A"

Hmmm...the plug even fits the thing on the black box that I think must
be the power connector!  And, if I can trust Matt Roberds's diagram --

>"Positive center" is usually associated with the small barrel plugs used
>to connect wall-mounted power supplies to all manner of small electronic
>devices.  You may have seen glyphs resembling these on the power
>supplies or the devices:
>
>+ ---o)--- -
>
>- ---o)--- +
>
>The first one is positive center (aka positive tip or tip positive) and
>the second one is negative center.

this thing is in fact marked "positive center". 

Now to go look at the spec sheets over at <http://www.allsurplus.net/Axiom/>.
It says:

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Operating Voltage (Sandpiper)		5 +- 0.25 Vdc, 50 mVp-p (max)

Power Consumption
Sandpiper				180 mA (continuous on)
Antenna Bus				20 mA (max)

TricklePower				down to 3 mA

Correlator Portion			40 mA

Sleep Mode				10 uA (RTC & SRAM)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
