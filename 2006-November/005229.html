> I like smaller sats with just the prn better than having information put in

OK, good. That's the 1st _legitimate_ complaint I've heard you make. Your vote

addressed this issue in another email.

As far as "having information put in two places", that only applies to data in 
the satellite table, which is (intentionally) hidden away in the 2nd tab by 
default, so I still don't understand your concern in that regard.

> > > The old one displays more information using less real estate.

Yes, there most certainly is.

> The old app put the same amount of information 
> into less screen real estate.

No it did not. "Less screen real estate", yes, but only slightly (31x54 pixels 
[WxH] less, in fact). "Same amount of information", no!

The old application displays a single (often truncated, with no way to view 
the tail, in fact, without resizing the main window) line of GPSD data. Mine 
displays over 7 lines of data at once (and it can be paused, and long lines 
can be viewed without resizing the main window, and it can store any 
reasonable number of 'history' lines [12 ATM, but that's arbitrary], FWIW).

My app shows whether the 'jitter' (or 'De-Jitter' as I refer to it) option is 
selected by putting the control right out there in the open. The old app does 
not. That's "information".

Furthermore, there is _nothing_ in that (hidden) 2nd tab of satellite data 
that cannot be discerned from the always-present, enlarge-able graphical 
satellite display.
