> The reason is not technical, it's political.  If we introduce a
> gtk2-based test client and drop the motif/lesstif client without also
> introducing a qt-based client, we look like we're taking sides in the
> GNOME-vs.-KDE war.
>

I think you're taking it too seriously. Anyone who cares *that* much,
can step up and submit some bsd-licensed code. It appears that Bill
Marr does care enough to have actually produced some code - I thank
him for his efforts.

Political machinations aside, I'd very much like to see a gtk2 client.
I have a metric buttload of things on my machines that use gtk/gtk2,
and I use windowmaker. It just so happens that my usual editor (vim)
supports gtk2, a number network tools (nmap, mtr) have gtk GUIs, and
golly-gee-whiz, gimp supports gtk also, so it's not an extra software
burden.

The embedded guys can still choose to not compile the gui, though this
means 2 new configure tests: enable-gtk2 and enable-motif both of
which are dependent on with-x11...

Also, in my few and brief run-ins with gtk/gtk2 it made much more
sense to me than motif. Gary mentioned earlier that folks knowing
something about motif are ... ummm... not a dime a dozen.
