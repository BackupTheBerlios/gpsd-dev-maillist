Actually, as you probably already know, application designers should never 
rely on color alone to convey the information, since users might not pick up 
on that (e.g. colorblindness), as you've aptly demonstrated. Any good User 
Interface Guideline should mention that. And, yes, I've worked with the 
occasional color-blind user before. To me the red/black text is an extra cue, 
but not the primary one, by design.

How precisely do you need to visualize it? Aren't the "N,S,E,W" markers 
enough? Not arguing, just curious. The only time I really care about where 
the satellites are is when I'm positioning an antenna (or the receiver 
itself, in most cases, since the antenna is often built-in in my latest GPS 
hardware). In that case, I just want to know where I might be blocking a 
particular satellite's signal with part of a building, etc. Knowing that the 
satellite is "SSW" (or even "SW" for that matter) is plenty good enough for 
me. I don't really care if its 155deg or 160deg azimuth in such a case (and 
if I did, I'd look at the table).
