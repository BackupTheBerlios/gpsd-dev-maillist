On Sunday 12 November 2006 10:08pm, Eric S. Raymond wrote:
> Marr <marr99 at highstream.net>:
> > An NMEA simulator is not that hard to write, really. I'm surprised
> > you haven't already whipped one up in Python by now, maybe with a
> > Tkinter GUI to boot.
>
> So, functionally, what does it do? I know it outputs synthetic NMEA; 
> what does it take *in*? That is, how do you tell it location-course-speed
> et cetrea? 

It accepts simple 1-character commands from a console to do things like set 
speed, set track (not "course"), set location (lat/lon), pause/resume the 
vehicle, etc. UTC date/time is taken from the host's clock.

I recently added 2 more hotkeys to increment and decrement the number of 
satellites reported in the '$GPGSV' NMEA sentences.

I never said it was sophisticated, just useful... in a minimalist sort of way.

I suspect that almost every hacker who's needed controlled NMEA data for 
testing has written something similar at one point or another.

Bill Marr
