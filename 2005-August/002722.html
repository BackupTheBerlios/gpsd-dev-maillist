Maybe we should not log messages that look like errors or warnings for
conditions that are in fact normal (like a probe for a protocol that is
not being sent).

Once I wrote a package to control a telephone modem, and it would log
error messages during its initial chats with the modem (while it was
trying to figure out the modem capabilities).  Every once in a while a
mail would come in from someone who had problems, and pointed to the
errors in the log.  Often they had even wasted time to figure out why
those log messages appeared.
So there is something to be said for not logging until RTCM has been
positively identified, and errors point to (transient) problems with the
data.
