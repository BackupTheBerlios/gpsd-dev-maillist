Yo Rob!

On Thu, 18 Aug 2005, Rob Janssen wrote:

> I don't think it should be indexed by PRN.  That could cause problems when
> fake PRNs are used for special purposes like WAAS.

Some of us like to watch the WAAS sata go by.  When LAAS come online I
most certainly want to see which LAAS I am seeing.

> The index I propose is the receiver channel number (starting from 0),
> where each element holds PRN, azimuth, elevation and signal strength.
> Zeroes are stored when a channel is not in use.

I assume that by receiver channel you mean the index in to the GPGSV
or equivalent data.  On my one channel EC-10X there is only one channel,
but it reports the several sats it is tracking.

> The "gpsdata.satellites" variable should be renamed "gpsdata.channels" and
> indicates how many members of the arrays can be actually in use for this
> specific receiver (value like 12).

A bit misleading since a 1 channel or an 8 channel receiver may be
tracking 12 satellitesa.  I have also been playing with the Garmin
binary v. Garmin NMEA.  In NMEA the sats in GPGSV are alsways sorted,
but in Garmin binary they are in some random order with holes in the
data.  There is really no way to know how the GPS is handling channel
scanning internally.  How about something like sat_index.

I have been watching the way one of my Garmin handles WAAS.  It seems
to report individual WAAS sat reachability for all the satellites,
incrementing one sat every minute.  After 20 minutes you have acquired
reachability data for all of them.  That should be often enough for
geo-stationary sats.  With the smaller 'channel' array there is no
place to store this data.  Not a big loss today, but I always hate
to through away good data, you never know when you may want it later.

In any case, it should be easy to do better than the way the code
works now.  So, show us some code!
