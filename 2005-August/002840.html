I have one of these. I played around with some vendor-specific NMEA
sentences (google for PNMRX). Some did work, others are dangerous. Do not, I
repeat NOT!, change the baud rate if you have a bluetooth receiver! It will
change the baud rate of the GPS decoder, but not of the bluetooth subsystem.
This means you will only get crap from your receiver till you take out or
short-circuit (very breifly!) the backup battery inside the reciever
(probably voiding your warranty in the process).
After this warning, the sentences that do work provide a lot of information
you probably won't need =) Among them is a possibility to change the
frequency and order of NMEA sentences. Unfortunately these changes are
persistant, so as people might want to use their receiver for other
applications that use those sentences, gpsd shouldn't fiddle with them.
