> On 8/5/05, Rob Janssen <gpsd at xs4all.nl> wrote:
>> I think it is good that gpsd saves the serial port status and restores
>> it
>> when exiting without finding anything, but when it has actually detected
>> a
>> receiver at a certain bitrate wouldn't it be better to leave the port at
>> that setting?
>
> So you want some sort of persistent storage of state?

No, just leave the serial port settings as they are when a GPS receiver
has been detected.  In the current version the port settings are always
restored to what they were when gpsd opened the port.  Better would be to
do this only when nothing was found.

Maybe that does not work for all ports, but on mine it does.  gpsd has
special handling to speedup the baudrate detection: it starts at the rate
the port was already set.
When I "stty" the port to the correct rate before starting gpsd it
immediately detects the receiver.
But when I don't do that, it hunts for the correct rate but sets it back
to the incorrect rate when exiting normally.
