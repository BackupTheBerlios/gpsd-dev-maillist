On 8/9/05, Belgabor <belgabor at gmx.de> wrote:
> Hi Chris,
> sorry, I didn't intend to offend.

No worries. I just didn't understand the problem you were trying to solve.

> My initial sorrow was (which maybe got
> mingled in the extended explanations & ideas I wrote) that gpxlogger.pl
> might miss a point due to accumulated intervals >1 sec.

There is that, but if you're worried about losing a fix or having it
be late, a perl script relying on 1HZ solutions is a sub-optimal
choice. Have your application poll gpsd directly, talk to the receiver
itself or instruct dbus to hand back results at a higher rate. Let's
just say that my development background is all about solving my
problems. I'm happy if my code solves your problems too, and I'll
generally share my code if you need some inspiration or a good laugh,
but I (somewhat selfishly) write code for me.

> I had seen the dbus based gpxlogger, but as it was relocated to the gpsd
> root (which I missed) I thought it was superceded by gpxlogger.pl ^^;

No, not superceded, just complemented. None of my systems have DBUS,
and the DBUS configuration is being uncooperative, so I figured that
there was room for a simple perl widget. That gpxlogger.pl lives in
contrib means that it's still sort of hackish and not ready to be
called one of the main tools.

> Going through the source I saw that the dbus info is mostly equivalent to
> the gpsd 'o' command, so DOP info is missing from (dbus) gpxlogger output. I
> will start a new thread about that.

Yep, I was reading the GPX spec, and noticed it had room for DOP and
SVs so I figured I'd try write track points that were as complete as
possible.
