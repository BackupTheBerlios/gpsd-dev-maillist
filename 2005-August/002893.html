"Eric S. Raymond" <esr at thyrsus.com> writes:
> Sometime no later than about six seconds after start, the SiRF driver
> should pick up a subframe with the current GPS-to-UTC offset in it.
> If it doesn't, there's a bug.

My utc-gps offset was observed by looking at the raw sirf data
(without gpsd being involved).  I just mentioned it in case "the 10
second" lag was really a 13-second lag.  It might help folks zero in
on the real problem.

But since you mention it, gpsd really could throw a few more
initialization msgs at the sirf gps's.  Right now it assumes the gps
is mostly set up to its liking.  If I run a program that turns off all
msgs but msg-41 (the lat/lon/alt position report), and later run gpsd,
then gpsd just sits there like a lump on a log.  It really should turn
on what it needs (including the 50-baud msg that it uses for the
utc-gps offset.)
