          (on a bu353).  What is weird is that a request for a
          1-second report rate is nack-ed.  Bumping the request to a
          larger number causes the request to be accepted, but no pps
          msg ever gets sent.

> NMEA:   someone (don't remember who) had a receiver that lagged 675ms
>         others can be off by more than 1 second.
>         reports on internet indicate that some receivers can vary the
>         delay over more than one second interval.

I wonder how many of these reports were Garmins.  Garmin handheld
GPS's (at least of the G12, G12-MAP, emap type) appear to have a
free-running clock that all calculations and messages reference.
Whenever this clock slips by ~2 seconds it is stepped.  This leads to
oft-quoted ~2-second amplitude sawtooth relationship between the
claimed time in the messages and the real time.

If the time-slippage is indeed smooth, I wonder if filtering the hell
out of signal will be good enough for ntp's purposes.
