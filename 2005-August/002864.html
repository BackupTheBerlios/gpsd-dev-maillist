Chris Kuethe <chris.kuethe at gmail.com> writes:
> This is only slightly different from gpsd putting the receiver into
> binary mode. In the case of gpsd going away suddenly you could be left
> with a receiver in binary mode. 

If that were the only concern gpsd could do another fork (after
daemonizing) and have the parent wait on the kid.  The kid would be
doing what gpsd is doing now.  When the kid exits or dies the parent
could then fix up the gps's setting.

> I'd be ok with gpsd configuring the hardware to get the information
> it needs. Document it of course, but isn't the purpose of a hardware
> driver to talk to the hardware, and do stuff with the end result of
> making the hardware useful to the user?

I'm actually leaning towards the "initialize everything" camp,
including binary mode, if it improves the quality of the information
that gpsd provides.

Many users are going to be unsophisticated and know nothing about the
trade-offs.  They are probably going to view the whole issue as
"gpsd's job".
