Looks like you nailed the ntpchm stuff in garmin.  Just did not occur to
me it might be a simple wild pointer issue.  Now tha garmin works
perfectly.

Now I am looking at a problem on another machine.  I have my Magellan
EC-10X on a host using basic, generic, devfs.  Here the GPS is on
/dev/tts/1, which is linked to /dev/ttyS1

# ls -l /dev/ttyS1 /dev/tts/1
crw-------  1 root root 4, 65 Dec 31  1969 /dev/tts/1
lr-xr-xr-x  1 root root     5 Apr 28 06:34 /dev/ttyS1 -> tts/1

Notice that the devfs sets /dev/tts1 to 600.

If I do "chmod 660 /dev/tts/1" then the EC-10X works fine.

With devfs it is not as simple as a "chmod 660 /dev/tts/1" since that
will not persist over a reboot.  Up till now I have always just let
devfs do it's thing, guess I am going to have to dig in to this a bit.

Maybe gpsd should just add g+rw to the device as root and avoid the
whole issue?
