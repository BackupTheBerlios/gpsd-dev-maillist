No worries.  I wouldn't even dream of dropping NMEA support.

The way that it makes a difference is this: the 'o' command and the
other recent bits of the protocol design are all based on the assumption
that, most of the time, the entire fix (lat/lon/alt) will be delivered 
as a unit with a single timestamp.

Older NMEA-only GPSes fail this test.  The result is that the timestamp
isn't reliable -- in particular, altitude can be as much as a second old.
