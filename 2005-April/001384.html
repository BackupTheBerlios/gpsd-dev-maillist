Yo Eric!

Me and my big mouth.  Always gets me more slave labor. :-)

The patch to swap units is clear, we just need something to hang it
on.  Not being a world traveler lately I am not sure how distros
outside of the USA come setup by default.  You do not want to hear
about all the hours I have spent in meetings outside the USA hearing
about localization issues: power cords, manuals, even whether ON is UP or
DOWN.  I am clueless, my nation is clueless, so let's get educated.

Let's start by collecting some data.

Here are the only Slackware 10.0 localization things in the environment:

LANG=en_US
LC_COLLATE=C

Older Slack has not even that.

Anybody else got something different?  Or ideas on how to tell those
that swing one way from those that swing the other?

We could always punt, there are the existing X resources "speedunits' and
'altunits'.  Maybe a button to set those for a user in his .Xdefaults?
