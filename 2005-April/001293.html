I would suggest that if the GPS is sending a "V" in the GPRMC sentence,
we have no choice but to assume the receiver knows what it is talking
about when it issues a "Warning" and the fix must be taken at least with
a grain of salt. However as we are in any doubt, the fix must be treaded
as bad.

On the other side, if the receiver is sending an "A" we must assume that
the GPS thinks the fix is good. You can second guess this forever;
agreed that knowing what the official NMEA definition states would clear
this up.
