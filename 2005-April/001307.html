Hello!

Last weekend I made some tests with gpsd 2.20 and Motorola Oncore GT+ receiver 
(operating in NMEA mode - Motorola has also own binary format). 

Here is preliminary report:

1) Model:           Oncore GT+
2) Works?          Yes, with some problems
3) Docummentation from:            http://www.synergy-gps.com/TR_Manual.html
4) Chipset:          Motorola,   MC68K  based
5) Interface:        RS232 4800baud (NMEA) or 9600baud (Motorola binary)  TTL 
levels from board, optional metal case with level coverter.
6) Vendor:          Motorola
7) Gpsd version:          2.20
8) NMEA version:    ?
9) Sentences:        GPGGA, GPGLL, GPGSA, GPGSV, GPRMC, GPVTG, GPZDA
10)  Notes:	RTCM input, no WAAS. In binary mode can deliver differential 
correction for another Oncore GT+. Similar Motorola Oncore UT timing receiver 
has less functions but better timing accuracy.

Problems noticed:

1) the O messages generated by gpsd contans "nan" strings:
....
GPSD,O=GGA 1113074816.00 0.005 52.162460 20.807093       ?        ?        ? 
330.3000    0.300      ? ?      ?      ?
GPSD,O=GLL 1113074816.00 0.005 52.162460 20.807093       ?        ?        ? 
330.3000    0.300      ? ?   nan      ?
GPSD,Y=8:8 77 213 13 1:10 57 277 30 1:13 28 104 0 0:27 70 72 13 1:28 20 171 0 
0:29 19 289 27 1:
GPSD,O=RMC 1113074816.00 0.005 52.162460 20.807093       ?        ?        ? 
303.6000    0.300      ? ?   nan      ?
....

2) xgps was crashing from time to time and failing with segmentation fault 
when attempted restarting. This lasted for several minutes and then normal 
operation was possible. This may (?) be caused by too few satellites visible.
NMEA output from "dead time" is in appendix.

3) Time display of xgps is toggling - goes to n/a when Y message is visible in 
monitor window and shows time when O command is displayed.
