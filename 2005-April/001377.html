Yo Andreas!

On Thu, 14 Apr 2005, Andreas Brauchli wrote:

> i'm one of the guys wondering why the americans haven't adoptend the
> international metric units (SI).. but i'm not here to flame about it.

Most Americans did.  All of South America, all of Central America, and
even Canada are metric.  Only those of us in the USA have not.  Everyone
in the world but Liberia, Myanmar and us in the USA.  How's that for an
affinity group?

> the patch also fixes the xgps -h output where "ft" instead of "feet" is
> shown..

I see two issues.  One small one.  If LC_ALL is set in the USA it is
more likely set to "C" or "en" than "en_US".  After all we invented
C and English, right? :-)

One big one.  It is rare for a US distro to set the LC_*.  It is for
the rest of the world to override our default stupid system of units.
So to keep us ignorant types over here happy the default, if LC_ALL or
LC_MEASUREMENT is not set, would have to be miles/feet.

Not sure if that would give the desired effect worldwide.

If defaulting to miles/feet does not work for you then my guess is you
are going to have to find a different way to tell us apart.
