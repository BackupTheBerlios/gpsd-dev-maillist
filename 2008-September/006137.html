> I don't understand PRN 1 in this context, but the others are clearly a "glitch" in the SBAS
> hunting scheme.
> It tries to look at those sats through solid earth, at an elevation of -91 degrees.
> After it has done it's internal calculations the receiver should know it's useless to try,
> let alone report them.

OK, that makes sense, but your filter is currently a little too aggressive.

I'm amenable to rejecting satellites with negative positions, but not
with positive (therefore possible) positions but no signal. And with
modern MYGPSPENISISBIGGERTHANYOURS 50 channel receivers, it's quite
possible that you could have multiple channels tracking the same PRN.
Or try to track PRNs that aren't there, just in case they suddenly
become visible.

With regard to the SBAS satellite positions, the SBAS almanac is much
slower than the NAVSTAR. So you could be tracking the SBAS PRN,
decoding SBAS differential corrections and still have no idea where
that SBAS bird is.
