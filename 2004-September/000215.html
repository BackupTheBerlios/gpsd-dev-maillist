> calls work when applied to a USB-device file descriptor obtained
> through open(2) on a pathname.

    From my observations, coming down through the kernel, by the time one 
arrives at node paths there is not much difference between /dev/ttySx and 
/dev/ttyUSBx.  usbserial core registers itself with the tty layer, and in 
turn it's sub-drivers go through the tty core like a standard rs232 port, 
but with help obviously from the usbserial framework.  The only difference 
is that a physical port on your motherboard is most likely going to support 
every feature specified by the rs232 specifications, while most usbserial 
adapters typically are different in what capabilities the vendors choose to 
support (and/or what information they choose to make available to 
developers).


    If we can get to the minor number information registered by a usbserial 
sub-driver with the tty layer, then the /dev/gps node can be created using 
that information and then opened for communications.
