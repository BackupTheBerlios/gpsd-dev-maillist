Just to pause & point out some useful info to the rest of the list:

TTL level RS-232 uses 0v and +5v, ordinary RS-232 uses -12v to -5v and
+5v to +12 volts for the signal (IIRC). The serial ports on most desktop
PCs will be robust enough to deal with either situation. I've known some
PCMCIA cards to be a bit more strict and only recognize "ordinary"
RS-232. Total pain to figure that one out the first time.

If your computer won't read from a serial GPS when it is known to work
with different computer, this may be the reason. A LED breakout box from
Radio-hell/Dick Smith/Fry's or somewhere like that helps.

As James pointed out somewhere, you can build a serial port powered
converter around a Maxim MAX232 chip. For further instructions, see
Google. (or bug me repeatedly)


Garmin handhelds (12XL,45,48,..) output TTL level RS-232 (and work fine
with gpsd, BTW).
