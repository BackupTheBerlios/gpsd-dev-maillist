No pictures?

The specifications say it is a Super Cap, but you've said it is a
Lithium battery.  You didn't mention soldering it back in.  Did you?
I'd be very worried about soldering Lithium batteries; they tend to be
explosive.

The slave labour camp who put it together usually also worries; and they
sometimes compensate by reducing the iron temperature or cross sectional
area of the join, which might create the problem you observed.

Gluing the item back in and *then* soldering is the right way to
field-fix such a mechanical design vulnerability.

A wandering Super Cap or battery inside the plastic casing could easily
trigger various components into undefined states, if metal to metal
contact occurred.  Permanent damage can also occur, and the evidence of
the damage can be delayed by months.

If you have no pictures, open it up again and take some, and I'll be
happy to review them and make further comment.  ;-)
