Sending 12/-12 volts into theTTL serial input of your gps will kill it. 
Encouraging anybody to hook up a TTL level serial port to a standard RS232 
port is not smart.

Anybody who would have to depend on the information provided here so far is 
not qualified to perform such a hack. Feeding 0/5 volts into an RS232 input 
is harmles. Feeding TTL level serial into an RS232 input is pointless, the 
signal is inverted.

OTOH, feeding RS232 serial out into a TTL input will create a miniature 
mushroom cloud.

It is okay to mention the fact that TTL level serial ports exist (mostly gps 
boards for embedded solutions). It is also good to mention that a converter 
needs to be used to marry TTL to RS232. Then send 'em off to Google.

Anything else (short of providing a complete EE course) is providing a 
dis-service.
