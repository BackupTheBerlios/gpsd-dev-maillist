On Thu, 9 Sep 2004, Russell Nelson wrote:

> 5) alarms can be spatial instead of merely temporal.
>   "Beep Beep Beep, if you leave now you can get to rowing in time."

Amtrak is notoriously late on the eastbound Capitol Limited, whose 
scheduled arrival into Pittsburgh is 4:40am. I have an alarm program which 
talks to GPSd and once it sees I've passed Homewood Junction, PA, it plays 
an mp3 at full volume. This means I can:
1) be sure I'll be woken up when I want to be, not too early to get as 
much asleep as I can, and not so late as to preclude me from showering.
2) call the person picking me up and give them enough notice to get up, 
get cleaned up, and come into town to get me.

If I were sick i could just have it call the person picking me up and play 
a recorded message, but I am not that sick.

It's sort of ugly, so i never checked it in.
