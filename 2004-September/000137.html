The intervals are configurable.  I'm yet to test, but plan to do so.

In the meanwhile, here are pictures of the FV18 module and my box around
it, for those who like hardware hackery ...

http://quozl.netrek.org/fv18/

The PCB is veroboard, aka strip board, with pin headers being used as
wire wrap anchor posts.  There are three AAA batteries underneath the
PCB encapsulated in hot glue, to preserve SRAM almanac.  The module has
TTL (5V) level RS-232, which has to be level shifted by the MAX232-class
chip.  There's a 7805 regulator mounted underneath.  The daylight LED is
attached through the MAX232 to the one pulse per second output, and
blinks at 1Hz with a 50% duty cycle, once the GPS has a fix.  Otherwise,
it just stays lit.  Serendipity; the data sheet didn't say what the 1
PPS output would do without a fix.
