> >Remco has suggested that maybe it's time to forget about this ancient
> >hardware.  Perhaps he's right.  Thoughts and opinions?

> For what gain? I know people embedded cheap Trip/Earthmates in their cars 
> and such. Maybe they just won't upgrade, I guess that's ok.

Getting rid of code that we don't have the hardware to test against is a 
*good* thing.  Less to maintain, fewer things to go wrong.

> As long as you keep passing [PRWIZCH] through in raw mode, which I assume you 
> mean to, I know of no reasons [not to drop it]...

Hah.  Derrick points out the should-have-been-obvious; Eric slaps own forehead.

Of course PRWIZCH will keep coming through in raw mode.  And of course
no old gpsd-using applications ever used PRWIZCH data in cooked mode
*because gpsd didn't pass it through!* :-)

Blammo.  PRWIZCH is gone.  Good riddance -- simplifies the gps.c code a 
bunch, drops some useless stuff out of gps.h.
