On Thu, 9 Sep 2004, Eric S. Raymond wrote:

>> 2) autostart gpsd when a gps CF or SDIO card is inserted, or
>>    when a Navman sleeve is inserted.
>
> Autostart isn't what you want.  I've given this one a lot of thought, and
> a conventional boot-time daemon that only opens the device when clients are
> active is better.   Why?  Because it can always report the last fix, which
> can be useful even if it's old.

Well, here's a tossup. See, on one hand, running the GPS eats power, which 
on a small device is bad (not *much* power, but if I'm not using it...)

On the other, I want a fix "already" when I go to use the device. My GPS 
receiver had cached its last known position when I replaced the batteries 
as I boarded at South Station, Boston. It "knew" it was last at 
Harrisburg, PA.

And lo, as we pulled out of New Haven, CT, I finally had a fix :-\
