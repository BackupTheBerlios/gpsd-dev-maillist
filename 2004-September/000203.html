Derrick J Brashear <shadow at dementia.org>:
> Well, here's a tossup. See, on one hand, running the GPS eats power, which 
> on a small device is bad (not *much* power, but if I'm not using it...)
> [...] On the other, I want a fix "already" when I go to use the device.

Answer: get a SiRF-II-based GPS.  These don't power down in the conventional 
sense on DTR low.  Instead, they spend 8/10ths of *every* second in low-power
mode. They only light up when it's time to ship an update up the wire to the
host, and do their fix checking at the same time.  

Thus, every restart is warm unless you actually unplug the sucker.
But they eat very little power.  The SiRF people claim that this trick
cuts the draw to about 15% of what a full duty cycle would consume; I
think one of the specsheets I saw says it's about 50-70 milliamps, but I
can't lay hands on it.
