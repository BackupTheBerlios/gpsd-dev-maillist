  A more recent version of my gpxloggerd is available here:

http://glebius.int.ru/tmp/gpxloggerd/

  It is adjusted to be buildable with the current gpsd snapshop,
and also features new "-a degrees" switch, that allows to suppress
logging when vehicle is moving straight forward. This feature
requires patching earth_distance() function, the patch I've sent
to the list earlier today.

To illustrate efficiency, there are several logs produced from
the same input:

http://glebius.int.ru/tmp/gpxloggerd/1.gpx - 1.3 Mb, the log w/o
filtering, as plain gpxlogger does.

http://glebius.int.ru/tmp/gpxloggerd/1a2m2.gpx - 335Kb, and the
trace quality would satisfy even queasy OSM mapper. :)

http://glebius.int.ru/tmp/gpxloggerd/1a3m3.gpx - 269Kb, and no
significant quality loss, yet.

Just open sample logs in viking or JOSM and click on the heavy one
to toggle its visibility.
