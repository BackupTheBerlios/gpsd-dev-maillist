> splint now lists 1689 errors, down from 1786 about an hour ago.

When I see changes like this:

<           if (!assign_channel(whoami) || !have_fix(whoami->device))
---
>           if (!assign_channel(whoami)!=0 || !have_fix(whoami->device))

it may be shutting up a warning, but readability and comprehensability
certainly suffers.

"!assign_channel(whoami)" conveniently reads as "not assign channel", but
what about "!assign_channel(whoami)!=0" ?

I have to stop and think if the ! binds stronger than the !=

Not to mention that apparently the first term of the || is being modified
to satisfy the checker, but the completely similar
"!have_fix(whoami->device)" (not have fix) is left alone.

Count me as "!convinced"...
