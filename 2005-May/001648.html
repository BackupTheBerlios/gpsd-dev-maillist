the information listed for the Garmin GPS 16 on
http://gpsd.berlios.de/hardware.html
is wrong.

* the device is switchable between NMEA 2.0 and 3.0 with the PGRMC1
command parameter 7
* the GPGGA sentence gives the HDOP (parameter 8)
* the rest of the DOP information is given in the GPGSA sentence
(parameters 4-6)
* as for the speed, I find it rather accurate. the thing is even smart
enough to report speed = 0 when my car is stopped, yet the position is
slowly drifting (when at a red light, for instance)

as for the "16 bit snr scale", where would that be ?

here's an example of what mine says:

gpsd: <= GPS:
$GPRMC,204005,A,4510.2337,N,00543.3335,E,000.0,000.0,130505,000.0,E*76
gpsd: <= GPS:
$GPGGA,204005,4510.2337,N,00543.3335,E,1,04,2.7,286.9,M,48.6,M,,*4E
gpsd: <= GPS: $GPGSA,A,2,,07,,,,,26,28,29,,,,2.9,2.7,1.0*3E
gpsd: <= GPS:
$GPGSV,3,1,10,05,23,224,00,07,35,096,46,08,00,084,00,09,47,286,00*74
gpsd: <= GPS: $PGRME,22.2,M,37.2,M,43.3,M*1E
gpsd: <= GPS: $PGRMB,0.0,200,,,,K,,N,W*28
gpsd: unknown sentence: "$PGRMB,0.0,200,,,,K,,N,W*28
"
gpsd: <= GPS: $PGRMM,WGS 84*06
