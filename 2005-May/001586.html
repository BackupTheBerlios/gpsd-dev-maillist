I can't reproduce this behavior.  Even when there is no fix, turning 'w' on 
gives me a stream of O=? reports.  

> another thing i noticed: starting just "gpsd" (without a device) then
> telnet'ing to gpsd works fine i get the P=? etc, but hitting "z" or "n"
> or "m" instantly kills gpsd - i don't know if that's normal :/

N and Z commands are working here.

> and shouldn't "gpsd -D2" (or anything above 2) make gpsd stay on the
> command line (instead of daemonizing) or is the man-page outdated? (the
> -s (portspeed) is still listed there)

Then you have a manual page from a rather old version.  The -s option
has been gone since 2.16.  The stay-in-foregroun d option is -n and
is independent of the debug level.

I wonder if you've somehow managed to mix release levels between the
gpsd main and the shared libraries?  That's the only scenario I can 
imagine that would cause misbehavior this dramatic.
