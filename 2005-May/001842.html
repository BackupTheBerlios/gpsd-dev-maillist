GPIO is a general-purpose I/O pin on the chip.  GPIO 9 is designated as
the Timemark pin when 1 PPS is output.  The pin is at CMOS drive level,
meaning it will only drive a certain amount of load.  If a board
supports the timemark, it may be brought out to drive an LED, or there
may be a buffer amp so it can drive an off-board load of some sort.  The
point is that the chip supports the output, but the specific board maker
may not wire it to anything accessible by a user.
