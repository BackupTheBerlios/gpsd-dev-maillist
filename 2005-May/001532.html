After finding a GPS device by autprobe, should gpsd try to 
automatically jack up the connection speed to 57600?

Pro: would cut report latency to a minimum.

Con: You tell me...

I improved the baud-rate-hunting logic tonight.  The first speed tried 
is now the current speed of the tty device, rather than the last speed gpsd
saw the tty operate as.  This means that if the tty is configured to the 
speed of the GPS, gpsd will never have to try more than one speed even
on the *first* hunt.
