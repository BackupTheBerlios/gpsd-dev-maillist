> > 10+2 ~ 13.  One is reporting GPS time, the other GMT.

> So the 'Z' at the end does not mean 'ZULU'?
> I agree with your interpretation, but xgps should then not claim zulu/UTC.

It does mean Zulu and the intention is that gpsd, and xgps, deliver UTC
(that is, with a leap-second correction).  sirfmon does *not* deliver
proper UTC, it has no leap-second correction.  Your 12-second difference
should actually be 13 seconds.  I'll document this on the sirfmon manual
page.

There is a SiRF-specific problem (documented on the gpsd man page) that
while the leap-second correction is currently correct, after 1 Jan 2006
it will sometimes be off until about 6 seconds after startup because it
will have to wait to get the leap-second correction from subframe data. 
This, again, is directly due to a SiRF firmware problem.

The O command (only) tries to nail down a more strict guarantee: that
you get a complete timestamped tuple of PVT and uncertainty
information, all from the same clock tick, in Systeme Internationale units.
