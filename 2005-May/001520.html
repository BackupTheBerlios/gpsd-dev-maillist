Yo Rob!

On Sun, 1 May 2005, Rob Janssen wrote:

> But you are probably using the current implementation that derives time
> from serial messages.

Yep, that is all I expect to have available to me.  I might consider
an inexpensive GPS/PPS for my fixed office installation.  On my laptop
+-10mS is good enough.

> A major problem with serial message handling is also that there is a fixed
> offset between the time in the message and the actual time it is sent,
> which often is left undefined in the manual.

Yep, the fixed delay is about 50mS on my USB/laptop combo, camparing the
fixed delay to other peers is just a matter of using ntpdc.  I adjusted
that out with this in my ntp.conf:

	fudge 127.127.28.0 time1 0.050 refid GPS

Nothing I can do about the +-10 mS of long term wander I see, but that
is OK for my laptop.  The few times it gets past that the other ntp
peers vote it out.
