  I've never understood the amound of time, money, and effort people
put into "chipping" the computers in their cars to get more
performance until today.  I upgraded the version of gpsd in my 2003
3/4-ton turbodiesel pickup to the latest 'svn HEAD', and the
difference is astounding.  My pickup now climbs in excess of 9900
ft/sec, drives at over 35,000 mph, and cruises anywhere from -50,000
ft to +50,000ft.  If I had known this sooner, I might have beaten
Rutan to the X Prize.

  Offhand, I'd say all the splint happiness has introduced a few bugs
in the Sirf code (if not elsewhere, too).  I'd try to narrow it down
to a specific rev, except that I cannot currently connect to
svn.berlios.de.  Anybody else having any problems?  I'll try hooking
up a generic NMEA receiver and/or a Garmin receiver later this
evening to see if they show the same bugs.
