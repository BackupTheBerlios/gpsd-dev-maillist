No, it turns out that the devices are all broken in the sense that there
is no hardware path between the PPS output of the chip and the
serial-to-USB chip.
So, when you have a USB GPS there is nothing that can be done.
I think it would work when the PPS output was simply connected to the DCD
of the serial-to-USB chip.  I have used those converters with a MODEM and
it worked, so apparently the hardware DCD is mirrored to the driver via
USB messages.

All my receivers are OEM modules to which I attached RS232 drivers myself,
and I wired the PPS outputs appropriately.   But I am certainly not the
only one who did this, I have seen several designs and drivers that use
this method.
Recently I found that the Trimble evaluation module wires PPS to RI (Ring
Indicator).  I will see if that can be supported as well.
