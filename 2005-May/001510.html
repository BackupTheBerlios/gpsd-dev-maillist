Oh, never used that one.  Here are mine for comparison:

# ntpq -p
     remote           refid      st t when poll reach   delay   offset  jitter
==============================================================================
- -SHM(0)          .GPS.            0 l    7   16  377    0.000   11.177   3.060

So you are better by a factor of 1,000!

> Of course when it displays values like 0.003 there is some rounding and
> smoothing involved and there may be a fixed unknown offset between the
> actual pulse edge on the receiver and the system clock ticks (caused by
> rs232 drivers, latency in the chip, interrupt latency, scheduling, etc).

Yeah, my jitter is a few mS, but my offset has a long term wander of
about +- 15 mS. ntp seems to be able to handle this in concert with
other ntp servers.  So I get the low jitter from the local GPS and the
absolute accuracy from the remote servers.  Odd, but it seems to work to
about +-10mS absolute apparent accuracy.
