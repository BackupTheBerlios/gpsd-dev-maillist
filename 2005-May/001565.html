> Is there a reason gps time is ten seconds behind wall time? Leap seconds?

10 seconds are a bit strange, 13 or maybe 14 would be what one expects.
Leap seconds are a possibility.  But when there is another offset it can
be because some receivers send their time messages a bit late, or there is
some queueing going on.

Did you try to run ntpd and add the SHM segment of gpsd to your ntpd config?
I suggest to setup ntpd with 2 or more other external references (like
Internet clocks) and see how much the gpsd provided time is off real time.

I recently posted a patch to use the PPS signal from a receiver wired via
RS232.  What kind of receiver do you use?  When wired to RS232 (vs USB)
please try the patch to see if it works for you.
