> After finding a GPS device by autprobe, should gpsd try to
> automatically jack up the connection speed to 57600?
>
> Pro: would cut report latency to a minimum.
>
> Con: You tell me...

I think we should be careful with any command sent to the receiver that
permanently alters its settings.
Users that install gpsd just to give it a try may find that it works, but
after that their original applications no longer work and/or their
receiver behaves differently from before.

When I found the problem with my uBox Sirf-I receiver not locating
satellites correctly, I also suspected that one of the Sirf-II commands
sent to it may have upset it somehow.
(I still haven't resolved that issue...  now it correctly locates 8
satellites but 4 are wrong.  there must be some firmware bug...)

When the speed change would be permanent (accross warmstarts) I would not
do it.  People expect NMEA devices to talk at 4800 baud.
