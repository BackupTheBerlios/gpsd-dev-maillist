> So
> you may get a message at 700 ms after the second that uses measurements
> time tagged 450 ms after the second.  And if some other task jumps up
> and delays things, that message may not come out until 900 ms after the
> second.  Things can get out of sync to the point that if you try to
> resolve the GPS time of our 1 PPS pulses using the navigation messages,
> you will find it impossible to be consistent.

If it offers some peace of mind: SiRF is not alone in this.
It seems that most GPS receiver protocols did not at all consider the
accuracy of time information.  Most likely they have only thought of using
it for some kind of wallclock.
Often the time relation between the actual appearance of time messages and
the times indicated in them is not specified at all, and while searching
the Internet I have found many pages where problems similar to the above
are discussed.

Fortunately we use ntpd.  It does not get disturbed too easily by time
messages that jump around a bit.  Of course it would be killing to use
simple time protocols (like the "sntp" used by MS) with timesources like
this.  The clock would jump backwards on occasion.

About the message 52: I added support for it in the latest patch, but
Remco's receiver (231.000.000) does not send it.
Does it need to be enabled, or is it only available in later versions?
