On Sat, 30 Apr 2005, Rob Janssen wrote:

> When the system is lightly loaded you get something like this:
>      remote           refid      st t when poll reach   delay   offset
> jitter
> ==============================================================================
> *SHM(0)          .GPS0.           0 l   13   16  377    0.000   -0.008
> 0.003
>
> i.e. much better than 0.5ms.  More like 10us.
> during heavy load this sometimes increases a little, but I don't see more
> than 0.5ms for the offset (jitter is less).

Are you sure you are reading that correctly?  I see a jitter of 0.003 and
IMHO the units are seconds.  so that is an uncertainty of 3mS, not 3uS.

Here is what my ntpdc doc says:

        Summary information includes the address of the remote peer,
        the local interface address (0.0.0.0 if a local address has yet
        to be determined), the stratum of the remote peer (a stratum of
        16 indicates the remote peer is unsynchronized), the polling
        interval, in seconds, the reachability register, in octal, and
        the current estimated delay, offset and dispersion of the peer,
        all in seconds.
        ^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

For comparison, here is my GPS-18:

# ntpdc -p
     remote           local      st poll reach  delay   offset    disp
=======================================================================
=SHM(0)          127.0.0.1        0   16  377 0.00000  0.002830 0.01543


So you are doing about 5x better than I am.

BTW, my ntp version is: ntp-stable-4.2.0a-20040617.  Never seen "disp"
displayed as "jitter".
