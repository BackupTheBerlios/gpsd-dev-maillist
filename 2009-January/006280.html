To expand on this a little:
1) this sort of thing does exist... commercially. Navsync has some
sort of indoor assisted-gps solution. ublox, sirf, broadcom (global
locate) all seem to have extended-validity-ephemeris and online
ephemeris distribution services.

2) have a look at the protocol references to see which receivers can
output ephemeris data. not all can do it easily, and some have better
formats than others. of the receivers I have these can emit
ephemerides: AC12, superstar2, trimble, ublox, itrax03, sirf2

3) it should be possible to break out the ephemeris meaningfully
without too much effort. that's only a 90 or 100MHz ARM7 in your
sirf2, and gps receivers have been around for quite a while... we just
need to figure out the smart way to do it.
