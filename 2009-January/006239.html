> also, since we were doing lots of cleanup this morning, i've just
> rolled a new snapshot - http://gpsd.berlios.de/gpsd-2.38rc2.taz

Watch for bugs near GPS mode switching, baud-rate changes, and so forth --
anywhere commands have to get shipped to the device.

I refactored the drivers in a significant way yesterday.  There's now
a method in most of them that takes a payload argument, wraps it 
in an appropriate packet frame with checksum, and ships it to the device.
As a result, gpsctl can now be used to ship arbitrary control strings
to a device.

Well, that's the theory, anyway.  It needs testing.

Eventually I want to merge garminctl into gpsctl and get rid of the 
silly little single-use setting-tweaker utilities in contrib, folding
them into gpsctl as well.
