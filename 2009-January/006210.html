On Sun, Jan 4, 2009 at 4:29 AM, Bernd Zeimetz <bernd at bzed.de> wrote:
> Matthias Urlichs wrote:
>> What kind of "other way" do you want, and why, given that both libraries
>> should be readily available on any system where you'd need them?

I don't see this sysfs thing on my bsd machines. be careful when you
say "any system" - we aim to be portable to more that just 47000
flavors of linux.

> The main problem is that you don't want to waste memory on embedded systems
> just to support Garmin devices. Walking /proc or /sys by hand is prone to
> errors due to changes in the pseudo-filesystems.

hence the use of some abstraction library that knows about the change.

> At the moment I'm wondering how the detection of Garmin devices works on *BSD
> and other systems - do they have their own kernel module, too?

AFAIK, linux is the only kernel with a driver for these garmin usb
devices... thus, *BSD would say "oh look, there's some usb device
here, vendor XXXX product YYYY. i have no idea how to deal with that,
so here's a generic device."
