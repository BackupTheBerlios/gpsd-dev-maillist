It seems that CDC-ACM isn't quite expressive enough, or many
manufacturers have bad judgement - on NetBSD there are a lot of per-chip
serial drivers, although some GPS receivers use it.

But, I think for gpsd there is a key difference.  Here's my take:

  drivers to talk to a USB serial device as if it is a serial port are
  the responsibility of the operating system.  This is either CDC-ACM,
  or PL2303X, or something else

  interpreting that data belongs within gpsd.  It seems there is some
  garmin module for linux that does more than just be a serial driver,
  and that seems odd to me.
