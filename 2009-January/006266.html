Matthias Urlichs <smurf at smurf.noris.de>:
> See above -- IMHO gpsd is the lazy party, by making special assumptions
> about the properties of the 'serial' lines it needs to write to.

There are points on both sides here, I think.  

Matthias, you are certainly correct that, classically, Unix programs
wtiting to serial devices should be prepared to handle EAGAIN.  On the
other hand, on modern not-so-memory-limited hardware the Bluetooth
driver really ought to be coalescing adjacent writes and capable of
buffering more data per second than DGPS ships -- Remco is right that
failing to do this is lazy and shoddy work.

On the gripping hand, both PVT packets from GPS and DGPS packets going
to them are datagram-like.  No one of them is critical.  So simply
dropping a short write is, I think, better than introducing a lot of 
complexity in an attempt to make the transmission reliable, especially
since actual reliability probaly isn't achievable.

I've added a brief discussion of this issue to the Hacking Guide in the
section on "Architecture and how to hack it".  Please critique.
