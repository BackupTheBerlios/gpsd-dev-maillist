> Yep.  Anything that touches DLE handling scares me.

Ouch.  That was me, commit 4458. I made a small change because we were
failing to get lock on an Evermore packet with spurious leading DLEs.
(that's test 15 in packet_test).  Chris's r4432 might be implicated, too;
I haven't looked at it, but the TSIP driver uses DLE-led packets too.
 
> > Once you get this fixed, please give me a regression-test load in Garmin USB
> > protocol so we can check for Garmin USB not being broken before release.
> 
> Not sure how to do that.  Being a binary protocol with kernel
> dependencies and interlocking commands is a bit beyond the regression
> engine last I looked.

A simple binary capture of 15 seconds or so of incoming data while the
GPS has a fix would do it (best if it's moving so we get a couple
different fixes and some nonzero velocity fields).  That way the
regression tests could at least check that packet decoding isn't
broken.  This sort of thing is why we have a super-raw mode.
