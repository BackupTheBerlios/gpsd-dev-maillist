Petr Slansky wrote:
> I have SiRF II chipset too, a USB mouse. I cannot switch it to binary mode
> with N1 command. I cannot switch it to binary mode with gpsctl command.
> I run gpsd with "-n" switch. I found today that this is important as when I
> remove '-n' switch, I can switch GPS unit from NMEA mode to binary and back.

I was also running gpsd in '-n' mode. (Recent 2.38dev svn r4687).

I think I have a clue what's going on. I played a bit an forced the
device to NMEA and quickly back to binary. The device won't switch
back to binary afterward. I restarted gpsd several times to ensure that
it wasn't gpsd fault. Even plugging the USB device in and out didn't
help, I had to wait a few minutes in between to get the device in a
state back when it emits SiRF packets again.

You may try:
 o to ensure that no client switches the mode
 o to plug the device out a few minutes to reset
 o to disable the device configuration from gpsd with the '-b' switch
