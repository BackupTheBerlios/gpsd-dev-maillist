> Getting a group together to fix TIGER (and the perl modules to access

Well, the longest journey starts with one step.  If we do not come up
with a means to share updates then it will never happen.  I have a DB
of 7k addresses.  Only about 150 appear to fail the TIGER lookup.  The
addresses have sufficient value I am going to determine the lat/lon
other ways and then key in adjustments to my local TIGER db.  Multiply
that by 100 other users and we start to get somewhere.

There are other projects working on getting gps tracks of all roads, I
am just interested in the geolocation of addresses now.  Put that together
with other's self interest and we might get something.

> Maybe the census folks need to offer a "TIGER challenge" where the
> team that corrects the most vectors each year gets the $1-million
> prize.

Moving the Census folks could take a while.  Seems odd that they even
have to undertake such a task.  Why is this not up to the USGS or the
USPS?  In any case, I say "thank you" to them and hope to get what
data they have given us in a more usefull format.
