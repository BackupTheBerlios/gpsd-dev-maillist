On Sun, 16 Oct 2005, Pascal F. Martin wrote:

> Basically, I found the Census Bureau very responsive and professional.

Good to hear.  They have taken on a huge task and done a pretty good job
so far.  Makeing the dataset available for free makes it even better.  I
could only hope that other Federal agencies where half as open with
their work product.  The FAA is only just starting to get it.  The

commercial resellers.

errata.  On a personal note, just about every map there is has the
streets near my house all wrong.  It drives me crazy and should be so
simple to fix if I can get in touch with someone that can fix the master
data copy.

> Like for software, fixing the original source is more effective

I agree, starting over would be a huge undertaking.  I am more
interested in some standard tools to use the great TIGER data in other

where are the algorithms?  Geo::Code is a good start, but only a start.
