The contract called for semi-automated realignment based on aerial and
satellite pictures, based on image processing and recognition.

The request for bid explained that the Bureau wanted to reuse the tools
for maintaining the database (new streets, I guess) at a lower cost.

The Bureau also rejected a (cheaper?) proposal from a map vendor that
had done some of the work already, but wanted to keep their copyright.
The Bureau required to keep the TIGER database free of copyright.
Praise them!
