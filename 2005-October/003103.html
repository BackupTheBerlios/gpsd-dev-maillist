Working with the TIGER database burns people out.  It's too good a
database to ignore (and what are the alternatives anyway???) but as
soon as you get too close you notice that every other vector seems to
be off by an annoying amount.  Sometimes the streets don't even touch.
(SF folks can check the Dumbarton Bridge.  There is a break of ~34
meters at (37.50661454 -122.11716362).  This plays some serious havoc
with routing algorithms.

Getting a group together to fix TIGER (and the perl modules to access
it) would be a wonderful idea.  The problem is how to keep folks from
getting discouraged by the breadth of the job of fixing the database
itself.

Maybe the census folks need to offer a "TIGER challenge" where the
team that corrects the most vectors each year gets the $1-million
prize.
