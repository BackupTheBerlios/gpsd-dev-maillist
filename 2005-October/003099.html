Gary and others--I apologize for not giving you more relevant details.

The information about what the Insitute of Navigation is perhaps is best 
obtained from their website at :

http://www.ion.org/

Here's my brief version. The ION is a nonprofit, special interest group 
focusing on navigation. The ION produces a quarterly, peer reviewed 
journal called Navigation. A very large contingent of that ION group is 
in the Satellite Division. The Satellite Division is focused by and 
large on GPS research and applications.

The ION Satellite Division holds conventions at least twice a year. The 
largest meeting is the international one held once a year in September. 
About 2,000-3,000 people attend. Academics, industry and military all 
attend. There is a trade floor where vendors demonstrate products, and 
give away cute little items. There are a number of technical 
presentations. The presentations are 15 minutes long, with 5 minutes 
more for questions. Each presentation must be accompanied by a paper 
that fits the prescribed format for the ION. There are template 
documents available.

Many, perhaps most, of the presentations represent research. However a 
number of papers describe applications of GPS. Some describe how GPS 
itself is operated and maintained. So there are no defined rules about 
what the paper has to cover--I think the ION wants to have a convention 
that is relevant to its community. If you look at our first ION paper 
for the GPSTk (it's at www.gpstk.org), you will see that it describes 
the project, and it's a basic HOWTO on getting started building the 
sofware. Our paper this year was an update w.r.t. the first one. I can 
send you that one by request but we can't distribute it on the web just yet.

The presentations are organized into sessions of related topics. This 
year, for example, there were two sessions dedicated to software 
receivers. Also this year, for the first time, there were four papers 
describing open source projects. The papers were grouped with pretty 
dissimilar papers into sessions that were not quite relevant to software 
development. So we are lobbying the ION to see if we can have a session 
dedicated to open source projects.

My goal with this email is to let the gpsd folks about the ION in case 
you want to present your work there. I know you are supporting the GPS 
community, and I suspect that more awareness of your work would be good 
for everyone.

>>This is the backstory. Here at the Univesity of Texas at Austin we are running
>>an open source project for GPS process called the GPS Toolkit (gpstk).
>>Basically this project provides GPS analysis software to the GPS community
>>that is LGPL'd. Also there are a number of projects that have spawned from
>>Cliff Kelly's receiver programming project called OpenSourceGPS. These
>>projects allow the user to control a Mitel/Plessy based GPS receiver card to
>>create GPS observatiosn. At the last ION convention, in Long Beach, CA, the
>>open source projects project met and we discovered a lot of common ground. We
>>are now lobbying the ION to recognize the topic of open source and GPS, and we

>>would potentially want to present their work to the next ION.
