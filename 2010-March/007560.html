Greg Troxel <gdt at ir.bbn.com>:
> It seems less icky to me to have a cached pre-built file in git than the
> switch build systems.

For the moment, yes.  But in the slightly longer term, this may be the
excuse I've been looking for to take our autotools build machinery out
behind the barn and shoot it.  scons has been beckoning for a while.
 
> Part of what we're running into is a cultural issue where some people
> see our current build system not running on mingw as a mingw problem
> (because it doesn't implement POSIX, presumably) rather than an auto*
> problem.

Since I think autotools itself is a problem the size of the planet
Neptune, I don't have a beef with mingw not supporting it.

>          And then the other cultural issue of people spending their
> time working on free not being inclined to accept any negatives from
> supporting non-free operating systems.

Yeah, I do have a problem with that.

Making life easier for Windows users is not really on my to-do list.
I *want* them to feel pain now, so they'll switch to something open
and be better off in the long run.  

I'm willing to invest some effort in Windows support, just as a way
of counting coup.  But the amount of discomfort I'm willing to endure
is sharply limited.
