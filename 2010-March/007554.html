Eric Raymond <esr at thyrsus.com> writes:

> I am sympathetic to this argument.  But, for reasons which are specific
> to this project, I'm inclined to reject it *on this project*. 
>
> Here's why: gpsd is unusually tractable to automated testing. It has
> no UI in the normal sense.  It performs a deterministic transform from
> a set of serial data streams (GPS reports) to an output (reports
> emitted to a client).
>
> Nobody here can have avoided noticing that I've put an enormous amount
> of effort into our verification tools. We have a large, effective 
> regression-test suite and exceptionally good facilities for strapping
> gpsd into a test jig and torturing it in a controlled environment. 

Completely agreed.  What I have experienced, though, is that the tests
sometimes fails -- and building sometimes fails -- on OS/cpu
combinations other than what the person who committed ran make check on.

So, perhaps modifying my suggestion, I'm not trying to say we should go
to "all changes on feature branch, with test reports before merging".
It's more "changes that have a higher likelihood of breaking build or
functionality on platforms the committer can't test should have an
opportunity for broader testing".

None of this is about what we ship as releases - freezes before releases
and people running make check multiple places takes care of that.
