That excuse dosn't satisfy me anymore, because: in the wake of C99 and
widespread SuS adoption, the portability issues autotools was designed
to cope with are significantly simpler than they used to be.  Fess up;
how many years since *you've* seen a Eunice installation or an HP3000?
The days when you had to (say) mine for vendor-specific variant system
headers are gone.

And yet, autotools has not gotten leaner to fit today's problems.
Instead it has bloated horribly. No, the problem here isn't "the
complexity it had to deal with", it was initial design decisions that
scaled badly.  Like building the whole thing in a bastard mix of m4
and shell *shudder* - even Perl would have been a better choice.

>          I remain unconvinced that the proposed replacements really
> solve as many portability issues.

I think I've mentioned before that scons on Battle For Wesnoth has
been a big win, significant because that's a rather large project with
