Chris Browet <cbro at semperpax.com> writes:

>> I see your point, but is someone expected to be able to build from git
>> on mingw?  People doing that is the only way to keep things working.
>
> Why not? Anyway, it doesn't seem good practice to have a piece of software
> only buildable on the intended platforms from the source tarballs. IMO, a
> source tarball is a "svn export" or equivalent, no more.

That's what I meant to say.

>> It seems not really sound to have a prebuilt configure, unless all mingw
>> systems really are the same.  I gather native builds on mingw are
>> desired, rather than cross builds from a POSIX system.
>>
> Actually, all mingw systems ARE the same. The windows platform as a whole
> could be see as the equivalent a single unix-like distro...
> Anyway, what would be your proposed alternative to generate the
> gpsd_config.h? (no autogen tools, remember...). I'd propose for gpsd to move
> to cmake, which is cross-platform... ;-)

So if they are the same, the prebuilt nature doesn't really hurt.

It seems less icky to me to have a cached pre-built file in git than the
switch build systems.

Part of what we're running into is a cultural issue where some people
see our current build system not running on mingw as a mingw problem
(because it doesn't implement POSIX, presumably) rather than an auto*
problem.  And then the other cultural issue of people spending their
time working on free not being inclined to accept any negatives from
supporting non-free operating systems.  (I'm not trying to say what
you're doing is wrong in any way, and I could see myself finding it
useful in a work context some day.)  Probably the above is obvious to
all, but I thought I'd say it because it definitely influences my
notions of what's reaosnable to do.
