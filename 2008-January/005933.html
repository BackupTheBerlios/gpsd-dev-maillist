  Also, I remember in college that there was a very simple calculation for the number of days in the month.  I couldn't find the exact formula on the Internet but it was of the form (int(Ax+B) mod 2) + 30  where x=1-12 March being 1 and Feb (12) shortened by the number of days in the years instead of the length of the month.  A and B where small constants like 2.3 or 4.7.  
   
  I did find this calculation at http://www.merlyn.demon.co.uk/daycount.htm#GML
  for (var m=3; m<14; m++) A[m] = 30 + ((3*m+1)%5 < 3) #13=January
  for (var m=1; m<12; m++) A[m] = 30 + ((3*m+2)%5 < 3) #1=March
