I find 79 hits for PRWIZCH on the net.  All the ones I've looked at
that give NMEA samples or specify a list of sentences reported also
have GPGSVs in them.  Here are the top few:

http://lists.freebsd.org/pipermail/freebsd-mobile/2003-August/001659.html
http://www.gpsnuts.com/myGPS/GPS/Hardware%20reviews/RandMcNally/rand_mcnally_gps.htm
http://s2.selwerd.nl/~dirk-jan/gpsdrive/archive/msg00771.html
http://www.ne.jp/asahi/gps/nori/Jupiter/1stdata.txt
http://203.141.15.20/50m_5el_2st/satk_test/0900_bin_ok_log.txt

So it looks as if the Rockwell chipset generated both PRWIZCH and GPGSV.
The difference is that PRWIZCH reports per-channel signal strength, while
GPGSV reports signal-to-noise ratios in decibels.  Practically speaking 
one is as good as the other -- even the Zodiac chipset manual I found doesn't
really explain why PRWIZCH exists.
