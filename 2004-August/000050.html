On Wednesday 25 August 2004 16:10, Rich Gibson wrote:
> I much much prefer a six hour old position with a timestamp to no
> position.
>
> The whole issue of 'offline' sort of confuses me.  Basically, we don't
> really have a reliable way to know if the GPS is 'online' or 'offline.'
> But we do know when we got the last useful information from the GPS.  It
> seems to me that the question of whether 1, 2, or 180 seconds is 'on line'
> or 'off line' is best left to the client.
>
> 'Here is what I know, and this is when I knew this' is what I want from
> the demon.

A gps that has lost fix is still 'online'. You get correct information from 
it. It tells you that it lost the fix, and gives you probably the LKG 
position.

If you pull the serial connector out from the gps it has become 'offline'. You 
get no information from the gps any more, although it still might have a fix.
In that case we should indicate 'no fix' and just repeat the old data.

The 'online' vs. 'offline' state is just internal to gpsd, and should not be 
client visible. The client only needs to know that the current position is 
old and not reliable.
