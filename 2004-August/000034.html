On Wednesday 25 August 2004 09:00, Eric S. Raymond wrote:
> I've got a trial implementation of client-output throttling I'm about
> ready to test.
>
> I've argued that the case for output throttling rather than buffering
> is that GPS data has a short life.  Furthermore, if you miss an
> update, no big worry because another will be along in seconds.
>
> Isn't this the kind of situation UDP was designed for?  Maybe it's
> a mistake for this to be TCP at all.

You can use UDP, but you don't have to.
Use non-blocking writes, and ignore write errors. The client has the problem, 
not you.
