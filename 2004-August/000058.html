On Fri, Aug 27, 2004 at 12:43:53PM +1200, Hamish wrote:
> I'm working on ships with the GPS on the bridge (front of the boat), and
> the NMEA repeating down a serial line 25 feet+ to a drylab at
> the back of the boat (nearest to the back deck & instrumentation ops).

Ah, I see.  So you'd really like to be sure than NMEA checksums are
being checked, and you have no ability to ask for retransmissions; so if
you miss an update you hope for the next one.

Since the checksums are per sentence, and the sentence lengths vary, you
could get into trouble if you relied on matching information from two
sentences of generally different length ... cyclic interference could
kill the long sentence and let through the short one.

Some ships are so big you can use a GPS at each end to figure out which
way they are pointing.  ;-}

I worked on a grain terminal programmable logic control system to VMS
interface once ... about 1986 ... only 12 feet of cable ... whenever a
military ship went past their radar caused our checksum failures to go
nuts.  Eventually we went for fibre optic to EIA-232 converters at each
end; just for 12 feet.  All fixed.
