Another good koan with which one may achieve enlightenment on this issue
is "What if two applications have differing timeout requirements?"

One addition to the protocol I have seriously considered is to include 
a timestamp with every message shipped.  Thus:

GPSD 1093554212.9,P=47.400569 -122.196612

The reason I've been thinking about this is that the timing of NMEA 
sentence arrivals is now the *only* feature of my GPS data that is not
passed through to gpsd cllients without loss.  (This could change if
I had a sensor that emitted a larger subset of NMEA 0183.)

Another reason to do this is that, if gpsd ever does turn into
a UDP service, the timestamps will be useful for datagram reordering 
at the client end.

This way of wedging in the timestamp would be upward-compatible with
the parser used in Kismet.

Thoughts, anyone?
