On Thu, Aug 26, 2004 at 12:33:07PM +1200, Hamish wrote:
> But maybe it is up to the client to hold on to that, as I don't see a
> clean way to have gpsd say "here is the latest values, but oh, they are
> 30 min old." especially when all the different values come from
> different sentences.

That's something that puzzled me about the gpsd to client protocol; that
the client could ask for a location but that the location was returned
without a timestamp.

The main purpose of GPS to determine a point in four dimensional space,
and gpsd is effectively reducing that to two dimensions before
transmission to the client.  

If the client asked for "d" and "p", the timestamp may differ to the
effective time of the coordinates; GPS data may be processed between
gpsd processing each client request.  If the client used "w" watcher
mode, then presumably the time would be correct to within one update
from the GPS.

The $GPGGA sentence provides the effective time for the geographic
coordinates in the same sentence as the coordinates.  For some GPSs, it
may be different to the time sent on the $GPZDA sentence.

No Use Case; I'd be running my client in NMEA mode if it mattered this
much.
