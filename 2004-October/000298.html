> Should I install gpsd as a service, with both /etc/init.d/gpsd
> and /etc/default/gpsd, the latter holding a 'start_daemon' flag and
> parameters?
> 
> This could be nice for people who have a permanently attached,
> permanently powered up GPS, but it might just be overkill. Maybe it's
> good for people who just want to plug in their GPS and have the service
> available immediately - gpsd should keep polling until it finds a
> device.
> 
> Any thoughts?

gpsd-2 is designed to be run as an ordinary daemon.  The new
gpsd is completely quiescent until a client connects to port 2947, at
which time it opens the GPS device and begins polling twice a second
for data.  When the last client disconnects, gpsd closes that device
and goes quiescent again.

Accordingly, there is no real downside to starting gpsd at boot time.
