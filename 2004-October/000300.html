Remco Treffkorn <remco at rvt.com>:
> On Sunday 17 October 2004 17:34, Eric S. Raymond wrote:
> > The new gpsd is completely quiescent until a client connects to
> > port 2947, at which time it opens the GPS device and begins
> > polling twice a second for data.  When the last client
> > disconnects, gpsd closes that device and goes quiescent again.
> 
> In deed, it always has been. The original reason was that the
> original DeLorme EarthMate was running on batteries, but only
> drawing current when open.

I understand that was the intention of the old code.  It didn't quite
work that way; there were some minor bugs that would sometimes mean
GPS close didn't happen when it should.

> So, gpsd only kept the gps open (on) while a client listened.
> (ed. There is a downside to it, and I wanted to make this configurable since 
> ages. Eric, any thoughts?)

I may already have done this.  See the -n option.

> Thanks for validating last decades design choices ;-)
> 
> The bit I was not aware of is the 500ms polling interval. Is it not driven by 
> a select() on the gps fd any more?

Yes, it is, but I halved the polling interval.  The basic send cycle of most
GPSes is one second; Nyquist's theorem tells us that twice that is the 
optimal sampling rate.
