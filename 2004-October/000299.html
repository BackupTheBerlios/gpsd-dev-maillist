On Sunday 17 October 2004 17:34, Eric S. Raymond wrote:
...
> The new
> gpsd is completely quiescent until a client connects to port 2947, at
> which time it opens the GPS device and begins polling twice a second
> for data.  When the last client disconnects, gpsd closes that device
> and goes quiescent again.

In deed, it always has been. The original reason was that the original DeLorme 
EarthMate was running on batteries, but only drawing current when open.

So, gpsd only kept the gps open (on) while a client listened.
(ed. There is a downside to it, and I wanted to make this configurable since 
ages. Eric, any thoughts?)

Thanks for validating last decades design choices ;-)

The bit I was not aware of is the 500ms polling interval. Is it not driven by 
a select() on the gps fd any more?
