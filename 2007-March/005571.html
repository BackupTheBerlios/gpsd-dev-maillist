> On 3/26/07, Russ Nelson <nelson at crynwr.com> wrote:

>
> That can be derived from hardware.html. Products commonly seen in the
> wild:
>
> FTDI FT232 (and register compatible)
> Prolific PL2303 (a.r.c.)
> Silicon Laboratories CP2101 (a.r.c.)
>
> Thos are the only devices I've seen. Sometimes the usb ids change a
> little (ie. my microsoft gps that came with streets & trips was just a
> rebadged prolific, ftdi has a new product that works with most *NIX
> drivers) but often the chips are register-compatible.
>
> I hear that the uBlox ANTARIS4 has USB support right in the processor
> (rather than a separate chip) but I don't have any more information
> about that.
