On Mon, 26 Mar 2007 15:47:39 -0600, "Chris Kuethe"
<chris.kuethe at gmail.com> said:
> I hear that the uBlox ANTARIS4 has USB support right in the processor
> (rather than a separate chip) but I don't have any more information
> about that.

Yes, several u-blox ANTARIS 4 modules have a USB interface built in to
the module, and the System Integration Manual describes the modules'
internal architecture and the external USB interface:

   <http://www.u-blox.com/products/a4modules.html>
   <http://www.u-blox.com/customersupport/antaris4_USB.html>
   <http://www.u-blox.com/customersupport/antaris4_doc.html>

Atmel makes the GPS chipsets that go into the ANTARIS 4 modules. They
have an ARM processor on-board the baseband controller chips, including
a USB interface on some baseband models:

   <http://www.atmel.com/products/GPS/>
