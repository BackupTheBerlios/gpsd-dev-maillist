> Michael R. Davis <mrdvt92 at yahoo.com>:

Actually, we're trying to make the answers be "no" and "as little as possible"

gpsd -> /usr
CFLAGS="-I/usr/include -pthread"
LDFLAGS="-L/usr/lib -lgps"

gpsd->/usr/local
CFLAGS="-I/usr/local/include -pthread"
LDFLAGS="-L/usr/local/lib -lgps"

Which means having a generic gpsd.h that is valid for any
configuration, and anything compile-dependent is opaque and contained
solely within the library or the executable. After some consideration
Eric is quite right - exporting compile configuration is icky and
should not be necessary.
