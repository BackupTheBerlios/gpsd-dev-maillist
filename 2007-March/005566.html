On Monday 26 March 2007, Eric S. Raymond wrote:
> Michael R. Davis <mrdvt92 at yahoo.com>:
> > Would it be better to have a /usr/bin/gpsd-config program like some
> > many other packages nowadays?
>
> Maybe.  What should it output?

I thought about this for a while. When you first raised the question what 
config info should go into the header (or gpsd-config output) my kneejerk 
reaction was: nothing!

After having mulled it over for a bit, I have not changed my opinion.

Gpsd is about hiding implementation detail from applications. Stuff that is 
part of gpsd (utilities, test tools, etc.) may have a valid reason to know, 
but clients should never care.

Even if I am overlooking something, I would much rather an application can 
discover at runtime what it needs to know.

It is possible that I just lack imagination. In that case, please help me out. 
What are examples of apps needing this kind of information?

Cheers,
Remco

P.s.: A gpsd-config makes sense as an indicator that the gpsd-devel package is 
installed, and to point to the place where includes and libs can be found.
