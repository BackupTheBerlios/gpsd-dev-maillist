Yeah, that's one way to do it. You could also parse the geodetic navigation
packet, look for a TimeOfWeek, Latitude and Longitude that are fairly close
to what's found in message 2 and use the altitude if those match up. You'll
need to do that because the nav type field will claim "no solution" though
there is some valid data in the packet. Yuk!

Another method for getting UTC involves much more insanity: working with the
50 bps datastream.

> What is SiRFDrive, and do I want to activate it?

It's their Dead Reckoning augmented firmware. It allows you to feed odometer
ticks, gyro and possibly accelerometer inputs to the chip to allow it to
continue to navigate in the absence of satellite information, and to improve
fixes when you do have satellites.
