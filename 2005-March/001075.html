The "real life" test gives
$GPROYDR,4,{TIME},{LAT},N,{LON},E,3,0,28,*6D

$GPROYDR1 looks much more interesting

$GPROYDR1,{TIME},
0.48006,0.33076,
{X},{Y},{Z},
-0.00000,
-0.05932,-0.12261,-0.07461,
7405.75435,29364297,
7,
*61

X/Y/Z is provided in .5f format (!), small numbers are probably
the v_x/v_y/v_z (should be compared with the standard NMEA messages)
and 7 - the number of visible satellites.
