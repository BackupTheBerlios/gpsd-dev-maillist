For me DGPS is still an important feature. The west coast is covered by DGPS 
stations transmitting between typically 150 to 300kHz. A DGPS receiver costs 
about 200 bucks, if you look. Some GPS have a serial input port for the data, 
some accept it on the NMEA serial port. For those support in gpsd is still 
critical. 

DGPS works, even when SA is enabled. It works well for relatively slow moving 
vehicles, not so well for missiles ;-)

Mariners are 'belt and suspenders' type of people (at least the ones who 
survive). I am thankfull for WAAS, but my DGPS whip-antenna will stay.

The US coast guard is still expanding the system. They are even keeping 
LORAN-C around.

http://www.navcen.uscg.gov/dgps/default.htm

I think the system will be with us for a while.
