> It's just a convenience that the system remains fairly close to UTC, and
> uses the same length seconds as UTC and can be fairly easily converted to
> UTC (which seems to make more sense to more people...)

What makes a lot less sense to me is that the system keeps time in funny
units like "weeks in 10 bits" and "time of week".

And also that some receivers don't make the offset to UTC easily accessible.

For timekeepers and Unix programmers it would be more natural to have just
one binary value representing ticks in some unit of time since some
defined epoch, with the offset to UTC already included or easily available
to be added.

Outputting time as week/tow (or even yyyymmddhhmmss.hh) is like outputting
speed in knots, imho.
