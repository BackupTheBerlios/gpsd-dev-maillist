On Fri, 11 Mar 2005, Eric S. Raymond wrote:

> This makes me wonder if DGPS support is obsolete and should, perhaps,
> be axed.  I know of only one DGPS reference station available over
> Internet, and it serves North America which is covered by WAAS.

For marine and aviation applications DGPS is still very relevant. At high
latitudes WAAS reception is poor or unusable. Up here at 53N, I'm lucky if
I can can even see AOR-W, nevermind get a code lock; pity the skipper
trying to navigate the Queen Charlotte Islands with WAAS turned on.

And regardless of the good C/A at L1 performance we all enjoy, if I were
trying to land a plane or pilot a ship on instruments alone, I'd want
even better GPS performance, no matter what the weather.

A complementary technology to WAAS is LAAS, which uses ground-based pseudo
satellites to transmit a ranging code, unlike DGPS which uses messages sent
out of band to optimize the calculation of the navigation solution.

http://support.inmarsat.com/coverage/coverage_maps_atlantic_west.aspx?top_level_id=2&sub_level_id=2&language=EN&textonly=False
http://www.waasperformance.raytheon.com/sis/sisqa.html

Oh, and I also shelled out money for the DGPS specs. Please don't let that
be wasted... :)
