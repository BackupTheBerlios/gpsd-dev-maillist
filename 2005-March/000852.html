> You need more kinds, and more pieces of hardware more expensive than any

> score a marine DGPS rig on ebay or at a boat show and hack on it.
> 
> It's not so much that DGPS is irrelevant, just that none of us have much of

So what you just told me is that while DGPS corrections mayy be a nice
idea in the general case, there is no way for *gpsd* to get DGPS
corrections without a custom patch associated with exotic hardware.
And we already know that WAAS and EGNOS supplies these corrections
in-band.

You're making a good argument for dropping DGPS support, there.  Not
that there's a lot of it -- 20 lines, total, in libgpsd_core.c. But the
good reason to do it would be to kill off another command-line option.

I can't figure out from the protocol manual what the SiRF default is for 
the SBAS and DGPS source parameters.  Do you know?
