Gary E. Miller wrote:
> After some research I have found that unless you have a PPS signal
> from your GPS that you can not expect sub-second accuracy and you are
> fooling yourself to use this as an input to the high precision ntp.

The Garmin GPS-16 (175$ at www.gps4fun.com) has such a PPS output
according to
http://www.garmin.com/manuals/GPS17N_GPS16_17NSeriesTechnicalSpecification.pdf

the PPS pulse has +- 1micro-second accuracy on pulse rise which is in 
sync with the start of the GPS second (which you get from the nmea data)

however, I have to find how to connect this thing to the serial port to 
get it detected by the ntp stuff. possibly on the "ring" contact ?
