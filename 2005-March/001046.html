Rob Janssen <gpsd at xs4all.nl>:
> That is not something you can do with a simple formula, it requires
> measurement data (tables) that may not even be freely available.

There is a spherical-harmonic closed form in NIMA TR8350.2
<http://earth-info.nga.mil/GandG/tr8350_2.html>.  Rob, would you be brave
enough to try implementing that?

> Do modern GPS-mouse type receivers output this information?
> I would expect it to be available only in standalone units that also have
> cartographic info.

Actually, many low-end GPS mice (including those using SiRF chips) emit a
geoidal-separation field in the GPRMC sentence.  NMEA 0183 describes it.
My guess is their firmware is using either the spherical-harmonic 
formula or a 4-point linear interpolation from a table of values 
precomputed over a fixed grid.
