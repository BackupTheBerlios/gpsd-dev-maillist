It's unfortunate that we cannot use a GPS in a USB environment as an  
accurate time source. The problem of message delays in USB is real  
but there may be a way to overcome it or minimize it's effect.

I'm thinking one approach would be a protocol that queries the GPS  
for the instant time to a fraction of a second. The outgoing delays  
on USB should not be dependent on the polling time and the store and  
forward delays should be constant when there is no other USB traffic.  
The jitter can be filtered out and it should be possible to factor  
out the constant delays.

Of course, this requires that the GPS manufactures change their  
firmware to add the new protocol but it's an alternative to saying it  
just can't be done.

Does anyone here know enough about USB to estimate the accuracy that  
could be expected from such a protocol?
