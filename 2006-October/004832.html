You're an exception.  So am I, albeit to a lesser extent than you.  
For 99% of all users, the use case for X is applications on the same machine
that runs the display server.  So, for targeting 99% of all users, 
$WINDOWID is good enough and all the frightening arcana around X tunneling
and permissions is irrelevant.

Thus, *in theory* X is all about network-transparent client/server 
partitioning, but *in practice* we both know that is a rarely used 
capability that most users don't even know about.  The few users
who do know about it don't require our coddling.
