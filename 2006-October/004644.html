A quick answer to a quick question while I ponder the rest of this.

On 10/24/06, Eric S. Raymond <esr at thyrsus.com> wrote:
> But, Chris, I'm wondering why you left out '&' and '!' and '?'.  Any
> of those three seem like reasonable introducers to me.  I might favor
> '?' over the '@' you're suggesting, in fact.  I'm a bit inclined to
> avoid '@' these days because it has become so closely associated with
> network addresses

Another good reason to avoid '@' is that it is the command character
for TrueNorth and Sony devices. As I'd rather not use an escape
character that has special meaning to another device, I think we can
disqualify '@'.

I don't like '?'  because v3 uses it as no-data-available signal.

'!' is nice, but you're already using it on the control socket. I
guess we could use '!' - we just need to adjust the control protocol a
little. Still, '!' is a rather common way to signal editors or scripts
or shell that you want to go execute a command...

As for the rest, I just got lazy and didn't take the time to see
whether a given punctuation mark gave me warm fuzzies or not.
