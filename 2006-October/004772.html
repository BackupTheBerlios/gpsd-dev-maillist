wrapper script will have to be the way we go.

> I tend to think that UNIX prefers small programs that do one thing well
> over big programs that have everthing but the kitchen sink in them.

So do I.  I wrote a book about this in 2003 :-).

The question -- and it's a biggie -- is what the "one thing" is.  You're
taking the developer-centric view that presenting through a given display
type is "one thing".  I'm taking the user-centric view that the "one thing"
is best rendition of GPS data on whatever display medium happens to be handy.

The underlying question here is whether we want to be developer-centric or 
user-centric.  I've been using the GPSD project to unlearn my 
developer-centric thinking habits.  I know this is not easy.
