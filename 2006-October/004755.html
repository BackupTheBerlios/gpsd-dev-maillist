Of *course* I'd do that!  Hmmph.

The reason I've been thinking of merging them is that user's point of
view thing again.  The distinction between cgps and xgps is convenient
for developers, because they are implemented rather differently.  But
from a user's point of view, it doesn't mean a lot.

What the *user* wants to do is pull up something that gives him the best
window on the data his display will support, whether that's a local X
display or a terminal emulator open to another machine.  He also
doesn't want to have to remember different sets of options for the
different modes.

If there's just one gps(1) client, that's one less bunch of 
things the user has to learn and remember.
