I love Unix programmers, I are one myself.  But this "patch a UI
defect by adding a flag" reflex many of us have drives me crazy.

Ship an I command to the device.  If it IDs itself as a TNTC, change the
the display labels.  DUH!

Note: I is only reliable when a device is actually bound to the user's
channel.  So just querying at startup won't work; you'll have to probe
the device type when you first get fix data.
