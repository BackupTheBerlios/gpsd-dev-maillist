On 10/23/06, Michael R. Davis <mrdvt92 at yahoo.com> wrote:
> The NAV Messages are send periodically to boot strap a receiver
> that has been off for a while.  When a leap second is impending the
> Leap Second NAV message is more frequently broadcast.

Not just to bootstrap a receiver, but also to keep it running
correctly. That is why there is the almanac and the ephemeris. The
almanac gives you a rough idea of where to look, the ephemeris tells
you where those satellites are. If you can wait a week, you can find
out where they were to within millimetres. If you don't get your
ephemerides, your solution turns into garbage.

> I feel that gpsd would be better off by leveraging a web service over the net when
>  it available and maybe providing a web service if we have the NAV data.

So once we find the UTC offset, we offer that as data we can serve up? Why not.

Or did you mean that we should somehow download this parameter from
the net? Don't lose sight of the fact that many gpsd installations
will never have net access. There is/was a linux powered commercial
car navigation system that was running gpsd to interface the SiRFstar2
receiver to their nav app. It had no network interfaces.
