On 10/25/06, Eric S. Raymond <esr at thyrsus.com> wrote:
> If these things don't all get merged into gpsctl, or until they *are*
> merged, could we at least have a common naming convention?  That is,
> I'd like to see sirftool/itraxtool/garmintool or
> sirfctrl/itraxctrl/garminctrl or sirfmon/itraxmon/garminmon rather
> than this babel of names that makes it look like their functions are
> completely unrelated.

Yup. I don't really care what they're called. Gary used garmintool. I
originally named my widget sirfproto because the only thing it could
do to a SiRF was adjust the protocol. My itrax configurator is called
itraxtool. When my Lassen iQ shows up this weekend it's configurator
will be called tsiptool.

> I lean slightly towards the "tool" suffix, but I'll let you and Gary
> thrash that one out.

"config"? precedent: unix ifconfig - standard tool for adjusting
different kinds of network hardware.
"ctl"? precedent: audioctl, mixerctl, sysctl, swapctl, apachectl ...
"tool"? precedent: none in /*bin or /usr/*bin. libtool ... feh!
"mon"? inappropriate. mon implies that we're looking in and monitoring
the hardware which is not what these tools do.

If I ran across a binary called gpsconfig or gpsctl I think i'd have a
pretty good idea of what it was supposed to do. I'd have to read the
gpstool manpage to see what it does. And we already have gpsmon:
sirfmon, cgps and xgps.

> > Once I get my itrax tool working, I'll refactor it and my sirf tool
> > into a more generic ... "protocol droid".
>
> I like the direction that's going.

Notice my itrax tool even has itrax_protocol_droid() ... how can you
tell I was watching star wars while hacking that.

For the moment, I curse Silicon Laboratories for not releasing
programming docs for the CP210x usb/serial bridge. It's making my life
difficult.

Maybe I should drop itrax for the moment and glue the SiRF and Garmin
utilities together first.
