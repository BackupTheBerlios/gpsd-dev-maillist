On Mon, 2006-10-30 at 12:48 -0500, Eric S. Raymond wrote:
> Chris Kuethe <chris.kuethe at gmail.com>:
> > Don't merge xgps and cgps. This will interfere with installing
> > pre-packaged gpsd on systems without X.
> 
> Yeah, like *that's* ever going to happen.  For our purposes the only
> 'systems without X' these days are headless boxen or SBCs run by the
> same people who are going to be using all our nifty configure options
> to hand-tune a build from source.

Let me chime in with a package maintainer's view on this: Please, don't
merge cgps and xgps, at least not into a single binary that will have to
be linked against libX11, libXm, etc. I imagine there are quite a few
gpsd installations where people like to keep a lean system, just gpsd
and their custom logging app or something.
<http://qa.debian.org/developer.php?popcon=gpsd> supports this:
Substantially more installations reported for the gpsd package than for
the gpsd-clients package on Debian (popcon is a tool, run weekly by
cron, that sends package installation statistics to a central database).
I think it's good to have a console-only client with little dependencies
on these installations, while still offering pre-packaged rpms and debs.

On Mon, 2006-10-30 at 11:53 -0500, Eric S. Raymond wrote: 
> If there's just one gps(1) client, that's one less bunch of 
> things the user has to learn and remember.

xgps was named gps back in 2004, and it got renamed to avoid a naming
clash with the gps application <http://www.gps.seul.org/>, and to bring
it in line with xgpsspeed. Reverting this would be a step back IMO.

Cheers, Til
