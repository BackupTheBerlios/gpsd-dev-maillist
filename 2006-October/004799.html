On Mon, Oct 30, 2006 at 04:29:23PM -0800, Gary E. Miller wrote:

> > gpspipe has two major use cases:
> >
> > 1) As a test tool (witness the role it just played in the J mess).
> >
> > 2) As a generator for shell pipelines or logger processes picking up
> >    GPS activity from remote systems.
> 
> You missed two:
> 
> 3) logger for local gpsd.
> 
> 4) as a splitter to grab NMEA and feed it back in to a system that
>    wants NMEA that looks like it is coming from a tty because it does
>    not grok gpsd speak.

  ...and #5, the ability to turn a serial port on your PC into a
genuine low-tech 4800bps NMEA-0183 gps emulator to feed other hardware
devices that expect a GPS reciever to be plugged in (in my case,
specifically, a Kenwood THD-7A ham radio).

  I'm also giving serious thought to whipping up an RFC 2217-compliant
telnet daemon that will feed NMEA-0183 from gpsd to serial-over-TCP
clients.  Handy if you want to run Street Atlas USA in your car, for
example, and don't want to have yet-another-GPS-receiver and
associated wires all over the place (and assuming you're as geeky as
me and already run a LAN in your car).
