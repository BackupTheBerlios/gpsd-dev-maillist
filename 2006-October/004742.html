Chris Kuethe <chris.kuethe at gmail.com>:
> I can read my perl 6 weeks later.

I'm sure you can, but you are a great deal more experienced and
careful than most Perl coders.  So was I, when I wrote Perl.  A
sufficiently able programmer can write maintainable code in any
language.

I've used more languages than most programmers today even know
existed, and I used to be proud of my ability to pound out reasonably
elegant code in languages even uglier than Perl (and there have been a few
of those, though not many).  But I tired of that struggle years ago.

Now I want my language to make elegance *easy*.  When I find one that
does that better than Python, I'll probably switch to it.
