The problem is a bug in the newer Linux kernels.  The tty layer is
mushing packets together.  Imagine you took 3 TCP/IP packets mushed them
into one buffer and then chooped them up on 64 byte boundaries.  That
would be a big mess and that is what the Linux kernel is doing.  If your
CPU is fast then gpsd can get the packets before the kernel mashed them
together.  My CPU is fast and unloaded so that is why I did not see
the problem.

I am working on a solution to use the GarminUSB driver in streaming
instead of packet mode.  I have Garmin Streaming Binary working for
serial ports, but not USB ports.  I have not figured out the magic foo
to switch the driver modes.  When I get that last step figured out your
unit will work.
