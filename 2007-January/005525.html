On 1/19/07, Diego Berge <gpsd at nippur.net> wrote:
>    I will have a look at MACM when I can get online properly and let you
> know what I think.  One thing in the meanwhile:  does it support
> communication of ephemeris data?

Not from the specs I've seen.

Ephemeris format will be a problem. Some receivers give you subframes
1, 2 and 3, raw (sirf). Some at least make sure the polarity is right
(sirf). Some give you a big array that's got the correct polarity but
is missing a couple of words (ublox). Some break it down into nice
neat structures (italk, trimble). A $GPALM nmea message is defined,
but there doesn't seem to be a $GPEPH.

To be useful, I'm afraid each driver might have to decode whatever the
receiver gives it into a prettier struct.
