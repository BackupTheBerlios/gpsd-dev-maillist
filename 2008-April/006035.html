Hey Gary,

>> So please fix the driver to
>> use libusb + /dev/bus/usb instead, or stick with the sysfs (where the
>> proper way is libsysfs instead of reading files from /sys).
> 
> The garmin driver just uses /proc/bus/usb to verify that the proper
> garmin driver is in the kernel before trying to open it.  Do you know
> how we could do that with the /sys tree instead?

PLEASE stop messing with directories like /proc and /sys. libusb and
libsysfs are your friends. You can't rely on having /sys mounted,
readable or whatever else. Also libusb does not depend on linux, it
works on *BSD and OSX, too

If all you want to do is to figure out which USB device is attached, I
guess the example from
http://libusb.sourceforge.net/doc/
is all you need.
