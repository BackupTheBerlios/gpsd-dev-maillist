Yo Chris!

On Mon, 31 Mar 2008, Chris Kuethe wrote:

> On Mon, Mar 31, 2008 at 3:44 PM, Gary E. Miller <gem at rellim.com> wrote:
> >  The garmin driver just uses /proc/bus/usb to verify that the proper
> >  garmin driver is in the kernel before trying to open it.  Do you know
> >  how we could do that with the /sys tree instead?
>
> creating the illusion of a tty is not gpsd's job, and you can speak
> garmin binary over a real serial line, right? is there anything truly
> linux-dependent in the garmin driver (beyond that crazy timing issue)?
> my openbsd box with a gps18 hanging off the com port should (in
> theory) be able to use garmin binary, right? (i think we now have a
> driver for garmin usb that presents a tty)

Garmin Serial Binary and Garmin USB binary are not the same.  I do not
see a way to support Garmin USB without getting in and twiddling at
the usb block layer.  Germin Serial uses a marginally sufficient
bit framing to denote packets, Garmin USB requires knowledge of the
USB packet boundaries to decode.

> I'm tempted to say "fsck it!" and suggest we open the device and try
> talk to it as if it was a regular serial gps.

I don't see how that could ever work.  To get rid of the Garmin kernel
driver would likely require libusb.  The advantage would be Garmin USB
support on non-Linux hosts, the downside would be some extra coding work
and a new packet layer to integrate with the current tty based gpsd
de-packetizer.  Since I use Linux that makes it all work and no fun/little
reward for me to code.
