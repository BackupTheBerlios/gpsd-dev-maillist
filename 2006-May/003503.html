Eric S. Raymond wrote:
> If GPS hardware were ideally designed, we'd get exactly one report
> like this per cycle from our device. That's what we get from SiRF-II
> protocol (all PVT data is in packet type 02) and with the Zodiac
> protocol (all PVT data is in the type 1000 packet).  These, together,
> account for a share of the GPS market that is 80% and rising in 2006.

interesting. that's what the antaris modules from u-blox appear to do
with their proprietary messages :

====

PUBX 00 - Lat/Long Position Data

$PUBX,00,hhmmss.ss,Latitude,N,Longitude,E,AltRef,NavStat,Hacc,Vacc,SOG,COG,Vvel,ageC,HDOP,VDOP,TDOP,GU,RU,DR,*cs<CR><LF>

example:
$PUBX,00,081350.00,4717.113210,N,00833.915187,E,546.589,G3,2.1,2.0,0.007,77.52,0.007,,0.92,1.19,0.77,9,0,0*5F<CR><LF>


====

PUBX 01 - UTM Position Data
The $PUBX,01 is a UTM (Universal Transverse Mercator projection) version
of the $PUBX,00 sentence.

$PUBX,01,hhmmss.ss,Easting,E,Northing,N,AltMSL,NavStat,Hacc,Vacc,SOG,COG,Vvel,ageC,HDOP,VDOP,TDOP,GU,RU,DR,*cs<CR><LF>

example:
$PUBX,01,075142.00,467125.245,E,5236949.763,N,498.235,G3,2.1,1.9,0.005,85.63,0.000,,0.78,0.90,0.52,12,0,0*65


====
[there's no PUBX 02 apparently]

PUBX 03 - Satellite Status

$PUBX,03,GT{,ID,s,AZM,EL,SN,LK},*cs<CR><LF>

example:
$PUBX,03,11,23,-,,,45,010,29,-,,,46,013,07,-,,,42,015,08,U,067,31,42,025,10,U,195,33,46,026,18,U,326,08,39,026,17,-,,,32,015,26,U,306,66,48,025,27,U,073,10,36,026,28,U,089,61,46,024,15,-,,,39,014*0D


====

PUBX 04 - Time of Day and Clock Information

$PUBX,04,hhmmss.ss,ddmmyy,UTC_TOW,week,reserved,Clk_B,Clk_D,PG,*cs<CR><LF>

exemple:
$PUBX,04,073731.00,091202,113851.00,1196,113851.00,1930035,-2660.664,43,*3C<CR><LF>
