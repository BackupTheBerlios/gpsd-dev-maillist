> Good point. My fault -- I meant to say "to send a client-initiated, raw NMEA 
> command to the GPS receiver".

No.  Bad.  Wrong.  Three-fourths of the point of gpsd, as I see it, is
plug'n'play -- that applications should never have to grok the report
or control formats of individual devices (the other one-fourth is solving
contention among multiple GPS-using apps).

Encouraging or even allowing applications to send NMEA to devices breaks
that abstraction.  Not acceptable -- but judicious and carefully-thought-out
extensions of the command set might be.  The NMEA string to be sent is 
in each case driver-specific knowledge embedded in gpsd itself.
