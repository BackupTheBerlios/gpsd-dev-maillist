> kind in which you continue reporting altitude wile admitting you don't

I think that filling in a reasonable but false value for altitude in
the receiver is ok, so long as the receiver sets the fix quality to
2D. Try and do anything more with the data than what the data says
it's good for, and it's your own funeral. That's what the DOP numbers
are for - an indication of how good you should feel about this
position fix. If you don't have a 3D-or-better fix, just point at the
altitude and laugh.

In gpsd, I suppose we can do the world a favor and output '?' for
altitude if we don't see a good fix. I'm just not sure which is a
worse lie: reporting 0.0 or whatever the gps gave us for altitude when
our fix quality is 2D....
