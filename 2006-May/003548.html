Yo Dan!

On Thu, 25 May 2006, Dan Oetting wrote:

> Since GPSd is already capable of computing the time of the start of cycle to
> pass to NTPd it would be almost trivial to offset query mode results to the
> time of the query.

Here is where your logic falls down. gpsd has never reliably known when
the sart of the cycle was.  The latency over a 4800 bps serial link and the
jitter caused by the variable gps cpu processing time is large relative to
a second.  I have observed  0.5 Sec jitter in the output of the start of
the "cycle" in NMEA output.  Interpolating below one second is getting
more information out of the data than is actually in there.

All you really know is that at the time in the time stamp of an NMEA message
that the position was probably close to what the message says it was.  To
get accuracy below a second you need to use PPS.
