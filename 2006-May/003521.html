At 10:58 -0400 19/5/06, Eric S. Raymond wrote:
>I should have said "Not passing the user bad data just because the GPS
>happens to be wonky is even more important than being neutral."  If
>some GPSes put garbage in this field when mode != 3D, then the O logic
>should report it undefined when mode != 3D.

Fair call.  I would say we should treat data in the following order:

actual fix data (i.e. 3D) > calculated estimate > NaN > completely 
random garbage

Bear in mind that although mode is reported as either one or the 
other (i.e. the receiver either has a 3D fix or it doesn't), the 
actual signals received and the resulting calculations are much more 
grey than they are black and white: 2D doesn't necessarily mean the 
receiver has NO idea of the altitude - just that the altitude is not 
known to a level of certainty where the receiver is willing to report 
it as "fixed".
