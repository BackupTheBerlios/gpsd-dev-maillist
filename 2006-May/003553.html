Sorry, but I don't have much insight to add.  The RTCM decoder was
initially written using the version RTCM 2.1 spec.  I don't have
access to any other versions, so I can't really comment on any
backwards/forward compatibility.

As to the NTRIP, I haven't been following it.  It would be good if
someone were to invent a sane differential-dgps correction format for
on-the-net data.  I don't know if NTRIP is that protocol or one of the
newer RTCM versions.

Ville Nuorvala <vnuorval at tcs.hut.fi>:
> After spending hours trying to find dgps-ip servers on the net, it seems 
> to me that there are a lot more Ntrip streams available on this one 
> broadcaster than there are public dgps-ip servers out there.

There were a few dgps-ip feeds back in the days of SA, but the people
running them soon lost interest.  The only one I know of right now is
the one connected to the antenna on my roof.

Adding NTRIP support sounds like a good idea from the point of getting
access to more differential corrections.  (I assume the NTRIP folks
are playing it clean and are not trying to patent the protocol or
doing anything that makes life for open source implementations
difficult.)
