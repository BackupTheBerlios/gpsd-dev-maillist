Marr <marr at flex.com>:
> One thing I believe you're neglecting, however. Certain GPS
> receivers (like my Garmin 'GPS 35 TracPak') can be dynamically
> (re-)programmed (via PC --> GPS NMEA-sentence commands) as to
> exactly what NMEA sentences they should transmit. If I (or some
> application) disable(s) a particular NMEA sentence, I'd probably
> expect 'gpsd' to react to that and begin to invalidate the unique
> (i.e. not reported in any other sentence that is currently being
> received by 'gpsd') content of that sentence. (Likewise, enabling a
> particular NMEA sentence should cause 'gpsd' to react to that and
> begin to validate _all_ that sentence's content.)

As I've said before: If you reprogram the GPS behind gpsd's back, you 
deserve to lose.  

If there is a legitimate reason to disable or re-enable sentences, that
logic belongs in gpsd where gpsd knows about it.

A gpsd user should never have to know or care about device-specific
control sequences.
