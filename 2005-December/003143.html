FWIW, I'm running Slackware 10.1 and successfully compiled gpsd-2.30 
(including 'xgpsspeed') back in early Oct with no problems (without the 
'Xm.h' addition).

It might be worth noting that I had already replaced LessTif with OpenMotif 
(from the Slackware 10.1 'extras' CD). Maybe that's causing the difference in 
experiences....?

Furthermore, when compiling a Motif app on Slackware (after having replaced 
LessTif with OpenMotif), it fails to compile (lots of messages like this: 
'Xm/Xm.h: No such file or directory') until I do this:

   ln -s /usr/X11R6/include/Xm /usr/include/Xm

That may have no bearing on this problem, but again, mentioned FWIW....

Having said all that, I see no harm in adding '#include Xm/Xm.h', since it 
presumably fixes the problem for someone.
